**Cyber Innovation: Reshaping the Cyber Threat Hunting Defense Line**

- Ruei Liu, Director, Cyber Innovation

## Key Points

### Introduction
- The 2025 record for fastest lateral movement by hackers globally is 27 seconds from the first endpoint compromise to the second.
- Single-layer defense strategies are obsolete.
- Effective defense requires combined network, endpoint, and interactive threat hunting to reduce Mean Time To Detect (MTTD) from days to hours and intercept attackers preemptively.

### Industry and Threat Landscape (2025)
- Attackers are faster, stealthier, and more cost-effective; defenders lag behind.
- Median hacker dwell time globally is rising (14 days in 2025).
- Lateral movement averages 29 minutes globally, with record speeds as fast as 27 seconds.
- 82% of intrusions involve no malware, relying on legitimate tools and credential misuse (Living off the Land - LoTL).
- Average data breach cost in 2025 is $4.44 million globally, with US enterprises at $10.22 million.
- Major attack vectors: public-facing vulnerabilities, abuse of valid accounts, and spear phishing.
- Taiwanese data reflects daily attacks, peaking at 37 incidents in July 2025.
- Top targeted sectors include technology manufacturing (+357% attacks), healthcare (+129% attacks), cloud environments (+500%), and communication equipment (+550%).

### Why Traditional Perimeter Defense Fails
- Assumptions such as "network boundaries equal security," "malicious files define attacks," and "single-point tools suffice" are invalid.
- Overreliance on signatures and endpoint-only monitoring leaves blind spots.
- Lack of cross-layered visibility fails to reveal complete attack chains.

### Case Studies of Actual Incidents
- **Case 1:** Multi-year stealthy hacker infiltration of key network devices via router firmware replacement and backdoors; undetected due to lack of east-west network visibility.
- **Case 2:** Strategic, long-term pre-positioning in critical infrastructure leveraging LoTL tactics, using only built-in Windows tools to harvest credentials and build persistent access for future attacks.

### Defense Solution - SecuTex Dual-Layer Protection
- **SecuTex NP (Network Packet):** Network-wide, full-packet capture with east-west visibility.
- **SecuTex ED (Endpoint Detection):** Lightweight, fast endpoint scans augmented with YARA rules from internal IR insights, filling gaps left by sandbox evasion.
- **SOC Platform:** Correlates and automates alerts across network and endpoint layers to deliver comprehensive attack visibility and rapid, cross-layer incident response.

### Results & Recommendations
- Post-implementation improvements for clients:
  - MTTD reduced from 11 days to 2 hours.
  - MTTR reduced from 73 days to less than 24 hours.
  - False positive rates dropped by 70%.
  - SOC workloads reduced by 55%.
  - Estimated cost savings of $1.9 million from reduced breach impacts.
- Recommendations for CISOs:
  - Conduct regular Red Team exercises to benchmark ATT&CK framework coverage.
  - Prioritize deployment of NDR to fill east-west visibility gaps.
  - Include cybersecurity detection & response KPIs in board-level reporting.

## Important Conclusions and Insights
- The cyber battlefield is evolving rapidly, with attackers moving at seconds-level speeds.
- Single layered defense models cannot cope with today's multi-vector, stealthy threats.
- Successful detection depends on integrated, cross-layer visibility combining network and endpoint intelligence.
- Living off the Land (LoTL) attacks defeat conventional signature-based EDR tools by mimicking legitimate IT operations.
- Strategic, persistent attacks on critical infrastructure emphasize the need for early detection well before destructive phases.
- Real-world incident data and tailored threat intelligence are essential for effective detection rule development.
- Automation and threat intelligence correlation help security teams reduce detection and response times drastically.

## Technical and Other Problems Emphasized

- **Fast Lateral Movement:** Modern attacks can propagate across endpoints in as little as 27 seconds, making timely detection extremely challenging.
- **Malware-Free Intrusions:** 82% of attacks rely solely on legitimate tools and stolen credentials, bypassing signature-based tools like traditional EDR.
- **Blind Spots in East-West Traffic:** Without full east-west network visibility and NDR capabilities, lateral movements within the network can go unnoticed.
- **Invisibility of Network Device Attacks:** Attacks on trusted network infrastructure devices bypass endpoint-centric monitoring.
- **Privilege Abuse and Credential Theft:** Attackers frequently use leaked or stolen credentials to gain and escalate privileges stealthily.
- **Supply Chain and Third-Party Risks:** Emerging focus on risks stemming from partners and subsidiaries as attack entry points.
- **Complexity in Detection:** Single-point tools see less than 70% of attacks; integrated triad solutions can achieve above 99% detection rates.
- **High Operational Workload:** High false positives, long detection and response times, and the volume of alerts overwhelm cybersecurity teams.
- **Insider Threat and Sophisticated Phishing:** Increased use of AI-generated phishing emails raises success rates of social engineering attacks.
- **Strategic Pre-Positioning:** Attackers embed long-term access points, waiting for strategic moments to launch destructive attacks on critical infrastructure.

---

This analysis highlights the importance of adopting a multi-layered, intelligence-driven cyber defense posture with integrated network and endpoint visibility, automation, and advanced threat hunting capabilities to combat advanced persistent threats in 2025 and beyond.
