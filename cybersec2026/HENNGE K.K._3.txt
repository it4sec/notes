## Key Points

1. **Challenge of Cloud Data Security**
   - Traditional security relies on drawing boundaries or fences (firewalls, domain restrictions, folder access, group permissions).
   - When data moves to the cloud, these boundaries become less effective in protecting sensitive information.

2. **The Fences We Drew**
   - Companies create file-sharing policies as boundaries based on:
     - Organization/domain
     - Folder/location
     - Group/audience
   - Boundaries become more complex over time, incorporating factors like path, file type, destination, shared date, and owner to draw more precise fences.

3. **Exceptions Create Holes**
   - Business realities require exceptions to policies, leading to holes in the fences (e.g., allowing partner domains, exceptions for public folders).
   - These exceptions reflect the need for safe collaboration beyond rigid boundaries.

4. **Fences Don’t Scale**
   - Static boundaries and manual policy changes cannot keep up with dynamic business needs.
   - Over-reliance on manual governance creates bottlenecks and administrative burdens.
   - Two prevalent patterns in Japanese enterprises:
     - Human Gatekeeper approach (manual approval workflows)
     - Platform Separation (using separate platforms for internal vs external sharing)

5. **Beyond the Firewall**
   - Exceptions are not noise but traces that reveal actual business workflows and collaboration patterns.
   - Security policy should evolve from rigid boundaries into adaptive workflows.

6. **Adaptive Security with DSPM + DLP + Organization-Shaped Security**
   - DSPM (Data Security Posture Management) discovers and visualizes actual data sharing patterns.
   - DLP (Data Loss Prevention) acts automatically to remediate policy violations.
   - An organizational approach allows continuous learning and shaping of safe collaboration paths by the entire team.
   - This combination creates scalable, dynamic cloud data security.

7. **The Future Vision**
   - Integration of DSPM, DLP, and organizational collaborative security leads to liberation and secure file collaboration.
   - The cycle of discovery, surfacing, response, and supervision reduces admin workload while adapting to real usage patterns.
   - File DLP enables effortless, confident, and secure collaboration in cloud environments.

---

## Important Conclusions & Insights

- Traditional firewall and boundary-based security are increasingly ineffective for cloud environments.
- Exceptions in policies highlight gaps that must be acknowledged and integrated into security workflows.
- Manual gatekeeping processes create bottlenecks that slow down business and increase risk.
- Combining discovery and enforcement tools with organizational collaboration enables flexible yet secure data sharing.
- Security policies should evolve from static fences to adaptive, trace-driven workflows.
- Modern cloud security depends on continuous learning from real data usage rather than strict prohibitive rules.
- Empowering users and teams while supervising by admins creates a sustainable security environment.
  
---

## Technical/Organizational Problems Highlighted

- **Static Boundaries are Limiting:** Policies based on domains, folders, groups, etc., fail to capture all legitimate business exceptions and evolve slowly.
- **Manual Governance Bottlenecks:** Human gatekeeping adds friction and delays in business operations.
- **Scalability Issues:** Managing exceptions through more complex rules becomes unsustainable.
- **Lack of Visibility:** Without DSPM-like tools, organizations cannot accurately see how data is shared or used.
- **Policy Gaps:** Business realities lead to exceptions that undermine security fences.
- **Excessive Burden on Admins:** Continuous manual decision-making leads to fatigue and potential errors.
- **Need for Integration:** Effective data protection requires combining automated enforcement (DLP), discovery/visibility (DSPM), and organizational collaboration approaches.

---

This presentation advocates for moving beyond traditional "firewall thinking" to embrace adaptive, data-centric cloud security with File DLP technology integrated with DSPM and organizational collaboration for scalable and effective protection in modern cloud environments.
