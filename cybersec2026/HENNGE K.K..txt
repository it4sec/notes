## Key Points

### 1. What is "Unintentional Insider Threat"?
- Defined as threats caused by organizational insiders (employees, contractors, partners) without malicious intent, due to mistakes, negligence, or misjudgments.
- Main forms include:
  - Lack of proper awareness: For example, uploading company confidential data to AI systems or personal cloud storages, ignoring phishing links.
  - Operational errors: Sending emails to incorrect recipients, wrong file transmission.

### 2. Why Does "Carelessness" Become the Biggest Cybersecurity Debt?
- The largest factor is humans; scale of threat = number of people × number of operations.
- Under pressure, employees tend to take shortcuts.
- Convenience features can inadvertently create security loopholes.
- Over-trust in internal systems or IT teams leads to low vigilance, which hackers exploit.
- Three major contributing elements:
  - Pressure
  - Convenience loopholes
  - Low awareness

### 3. Market Alerts and Cyber Insurance Trends
- Regulatory bodies encourage companies to evaluate cybersecurity risks and consider cyber insurance.
- Cyber insurance market demand has grown drastically (6x increase in 5 years in Taiwan, global market expected to reach $29 billion by 2027).
- Common requirements for insurance:
  - Multi-factor authentication
  - Endpoint detection and response
  - Email security
  - Immutable, isolated backups
  - Incident response plan
  - Employee security training
  - Privileged access management
  - Patch management

### 4. How HENNGE Helps Prevent Damage from "Carelessness"
- HENNGE One provides comprehensive cloud security covering identity verification and data leakage prevention.
- Protection workflow includes:
  - Detecting and blocking phishing and malicious attacks.
  - Social engineering training and simulation.
  - Identity and credential protection.
  - Data Leakage Protection (DLP) across email and cloud file sharing.
- Specific features:
  - Email DLP: Detects and pauses suspicious emails, allows recall before files/downloads are accessed.
  - File DLP: Monitors and controls cloud file-sharing permissions to prevent excessive external sharing.
  - Social engineering training platform (Tadrill): Simulates phishing attacks, collects incident reports, and provides educational courses to raise employee awareness.

## Important Conclusions and Insights
- Humans are a major asset but also the largest cybersecurity vulnerability.
- The scale of unintentional insider threats grows with the number of employees and daily operations.
- Pressure, system convenience, and low vigilance compound the risk of accidental data breaches.
- Cyber insurance now requires employee security training as a mandatory condition.
- Combining data leakage protection and social engineering simulations significantly reduces risks from internal mistakes.
- Preventive measures focus on a holistic approach addressing technology, human behavior, and education.

---

## Technical or Other Problems Highlighted by the Presentation

- **Unintentional Data Leakage:** Employees inadvertently expose confidential data by misusing AI platforms (e.g., uploading to ChatGPT), misconfiguring email recipients, or sharing cloud files externally without proper controls.
- **Insufficient Awareness and Training:** Lack of clear policies and employee understanding causes risky behaviors.
- **Security Loopholes from Convenience Features:** Auto-complete functions, easy file sharing, and low-friction email sending increase the chance of mistakes.
- **Pressure-Induced Shortcuts:** Work pressures lead to bypassing security protocols.
- **Low Employee Vigilance/Over-Trust:** Employees tend to trust internal systems and IT, making them susceptible to social engineering.
- **Lack of Visibility and Control:** Without robust monitoring tools, organizations struggle to detect and remediate data leaks promptly.
- **Growing Demand for Cyber Insurance:** Organizations face challenges meeting insurer requirements that demand comprehensive security controls, training, and incident preparedness. Failure to comply raises insurance denial risks.

---

This presentation underscores the critical necessity of addressing "unintentional insider threats" through a combination of technology solutions, employee training, and thoughtful policy to manage cybersecurity risks effectively in modern enterprises.
