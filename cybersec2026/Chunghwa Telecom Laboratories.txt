### Title
Using Open Technologies to Build Practical LLM Compliance Protection Chain  
用開放技術打造可落地的 LLM 合規防護鏈

### Author/Presenter
曲華榮 (Qu Huarong)  
Senior Researcher, Chunghwa Telecom Research Institute

---

## Key Points

- **Current State of AI Safety and Compliance**  
  Prompt injection remains the primary security risk for Large Language Models (LLMs). Safety guardrail technologies are implemented to add policy and security constraints to model inputs and outputs, supplementing model-level safeguards.

- **Evolution of LLM Safety Guard Models**  
  Timeline for key LLM-based safety guard advancements:
  - Meta: Llama Guard (Dec 2023)
  - Google: ShieldGemma (July 2024)
  - OpenAI: gpt-oss-safeguard (Aug 2025)
  - Alibaba: Qwen3Guard (Sept 2025)
  - MediaTek: Research Breeze Guard (March 2026)
  - Chunghwa Telecom Laboratories: TWGuard (April 2026)

- **Comprehensive Safety Compliance Chain Trends**  
  Integration across AI architecture including:
  - Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) frameworks for embedding, vector database, and reranking
  - Agentic workflow for planning, tool use (API calls), and self-reflection to improve output quality
  - Data governance for continuous training, including prompt-response logging, AI QA evaluation, and model fine-tuning

- **Compliance Risk Cases**  
  1. **Secrets Leakage** – Unintentional exposure of sensitive credentials through RAG-based systems  
  2. **Personally Identifiable Information (PII) Protection** – Prevent leakage of personal data in AI service logs and training pipelines  
  3. **Hallucination in Agentic Workflows** – Ensuring factual correctness of LLM outputs, especially in enterprise workflows like ERP

- **Technical Solutions Introduced**  
  - **Detect-Secrets (Yelp)** for scanning and redacting high-entropy secrets before model processing  
  - **Microsoft Presidio** for PII recognition and anonymization conforming to GDPR/HIPAA  
  - **OpenAI Privacy Filter** for dual masking of PII and secrets with high precision  
  - **IBM Granite Guardian** for hallucination detection in LLM-generated responses, checking grounding against source data  

- **Emerging Threat in Autonomous AI Agents**  
  Discussed OpenClaw architecture vulnerabilities and introduced agent-threat-rules (ATR) for rule-based detection, as well as LLM-driven pattern analysis for security.

---

## Important Conclusions and Insights

- The deployment of LLM safety and compliance requires a full-stack approach that spans beyond just model-level protection into entire AI system architectures.
- Sensitive data such as secrets and PII must be intercepted and sanitized before reaching the model or being logged for training to prevent leaks.
- Hallucination detection is crucial in agentic workflows to maintain output factuality and prevent costly business errors.
- Open and neutral technologies facilitate the building of comprehensive and domain-tailored AI compliance protection chains.
- Integration of RAG, agentic workflows, and continuous training with risk detection models represents the future of practical LLM compliance solutions.
- Autonomous AI agents introduce novel security risks that require specialized threat detection frameworks combining rule-based and LLM-driven methods.

---

## Technical and Other Problems Emphasized

- **Prompt Injection Vulnerability**: LLMs remain susceptible to prompt injection attacks which can bypass safety filters causing illicit or harmful outputs.
- **Secrets Leakage in Enterprise RAG Systems**: Confidential keys, passwords, and tokens mixed with large enterprise datasets can accidentally be leaked if filtering or access controls are insufficient.
- **PII Exposure via Training Data**: Customer personal data included in prompts and logs can be exposed to unauthorized parties or external annotation vendors when collected for model retraining.
- **LLM Hallucination in Automated Workflows**: False or fabricated information generated by models, unnoticed by users, poses a business risk in professional domains like procurement.
- **Complexity of Data Governance**: Filtering and sanitizing sensitive fields within massive data lakes for AI use is operationally heavy and challenging.
- **Scalability vs. Precision Trade-offs**: Transformer-based privacy filters offer high precision at the cost of computational resources and latency.
- **Risk of Autonomous Agent Exploitation**: Multi-agent AI systems can be compromised through malicious skill manipulation, privilege abuse, or rogue agent behavior, requiring advanced detection and mitigation.
