## Key Points

### Accelerating Cybersecurity Pressures
- **Three main cyber pressures:**
  - Application explosion leading to complexity.
  - AI versus AI in attack-defense dynamics.
  - Quantum threats with uncertainty around Q-day arrival.
- Enterprises have limited time to prepare for emerging quantum threats.

### Challenges in Hybrid Environments
- Distributed services and complex devices create difficult management environments.
- Challenges include inconsistent security policies, fragmented visibility, and poor connectivity experience.

### FortiGate as the Core AI Protector
- Features a centralized command center for strategy and coordination.
- Real-time defense execution with rapid response and integrated security across all boundaries.

### Hybrid Mesh Firewall (HMF)
- Fortinet's solution for modern dispersed networks.
- Single platform for on-premises and cloud firewalls.
- Unified management and risk analysis with AI-driven intelligent threat protection.
- Supports a broad range of virtual firewalls across cloud providers (AWS, Azure, Oracle, GCP, private cloud).

### Proprietary ASIC Technology
- Combines network processor (NP7), security processor (SP5), and content processor (CP9) for unmatched performance.
- ASIC boosts throughput and reduces power consumption in key areas such as IPS, AV, firewall encryption, and SD-WAN.

### FortiOS 8 Enhancements
- Expanded AI application governance and data protection.
- Integrates OCR with DLP for enhanced data leakage prevention.
- Introduces AI-assisted management for future-proofing against encryption evolution and quantum threats.

### FortiGuard AI-Driven Security Services
- Massive detection scope spanning millions of firewalls, endpoints, emails, and sandbox files.
- Active detection, rapid response, and comprehensive protection across Fortinet’s security fabric.
- AI/ML engines improve detection of evasive malware (e.g., Cobalt Strike), phishing, and sophisticated threats.

### FortiAI Operational Assistant
- Accelerates understanding of products and processes using natural language.
- Enables simplified and faster firewall configuration via graphical inputs.
- Supports rapid security operations center (SOC) event analysis and automated rule creation via natural language interaction.

  - Quantum Key Distribution (QKD).
  - Post-Quantum Cryptography (PQC) algorithms such as Crystals Kyber, ML-KEM, BIKE, HQC, Frodo, etc.
  - Hybrid cryptography modes and algorithm stacking for resilience.
- Timeline includes mandatory adoption phases between 2027-2035.

## Technical and Other Problems Emphasized in the Presentation

### Problems Highlighted
- The explosive growth of applications complicates security management due to heterogeneity and volume.
- AI-driven attackers require defenders to leverage AI for threat detection, analysis, and response.
- The quantum computing era threatens the foundation of current encryption algorithms, creating urgency for migration to quantum-resistant technologies.
- Fragmented management in hybrid environments results in inconsistent policies, reduced visibility, and operational inefficiencies.
- Shadow AI (unauthorized AI usage by employees) presents new risks in data security and compliance.
- Handling zero-day threats demands continued advances in AI-driven sandboxing and inline malware defenses.
- Complexity and scale of modern networks make manual firewall configuration and security analysis slow and error-prone.
- Meeting emerging compliance mandates like CNSA 2.0 requires cryptographic agility and system upgrades ahead of deadlines.
