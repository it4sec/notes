## Title
AIOT and Satellite Communications in Situational Awareness and Unmanned Systems Applications  
(AIOT與衛星通訊在態勢感知與無人系統中的應用)

## Authors/Presenters

## Key Points

### Leading Edge AI Computing by ADLINK
- ADLINK provides rugged AI computing solutions designed for mission-critical applications.
- Systems are vertically certified (MIL-STD-810 and EN50155 compliant).
- Key features include ruggedness (shock, vibration, wide temperature stability), long lifecycle (up to 10 years), and customizable chassis/backplanes.
- ADLINK emphasizes real-time and secure intelligence for edge AI agents.

### Satellite Communications: Market Context and Trends
- Low Earth Orbit (LEO) satellites are rapidly growing, with projections to reach a $780 billion market by 2035 (7.97% CAGR).
- IoT market linked to satellite connectivity estimated at $15.77 billion with 24.1% CAGR.
- Satellite population is growing crowded, dominated by players like SpaceX Starlink (77% of active satellites) and OneWeb.
- Increased satellite activity is driven by the need to connect the unconnected: 6.1 billion mobile connections covered by satellites/non-terrestrial networks (NTNs).
- Connectivity covers remote, mobile, and secure environments.

### Technological Evolutions in Satellite and IoT Networks
- IoT universe expected to reach ~2.5 billion devices within satellite addressable area.
- Key sectors: automotive, utilities, agriculture, manufacturing, oil and gas.
- Advancements include 5G non-terrestrial networks (NTN), software-defined, scalable, and open AI network automation, and enhanced encryption mechanisms.

### Satellite Security and Resilience Platform
- Multi-orbit (GEO, MEO, LEO), ubiquitous quantum-resistant connectivity.
- Network management includes zero-trust architecture (ZTA), IoT management, network gateways, PQC (post-quantum cryptography) secure tunnels.
- Physical AI supports secure edge operations.

### Edge AI Automation and Unmanned Vehicle Collaboration
- Integration of high-precision control, real-time data processing, and edge AI allows autonomous missions in complex or extreme environments.
- Stable and wide-area connectivity achieved by combining 5G with satellite communications.
- Security ensured by zero-trust architecture (ZTA) and PQC.
- Systems designed for continuous operation under network disconnections or high-risk environments.
- Key unmanned vehicle security elements: GPS/GNSS, Camera/LiDAR, core IPC, display, battery power, encrypted communications, secure bootloader, and hardware security modules (HSM).

## Important Conclusions and Insights
- The convergence of AIOT with satellite communications offers reliable, secure, and resilient solutions for situational awareness and autonomous unmanned systems.
- Rugged AI computing platforms are essential for mission-critical success in harsh environments.
- The explosive growth of satellite infrastructure, especially in LEO, supports the expansion and scalability of IoT and mobile connectivity worldwide.
- Security at both the network and edge-device levels, such as PQC and zero trust, is fundamental for operational reliability.
- Autonomous systems leveraging these technologies can provide enhanced efficiency, safety, and operational continuity in remote and adverse environments.

---

## Technical and Other Problems Highlighted
- The space environment is becoming increasingly crowded with satellites, which may pose challenges for managing orbital debris and interference.
- Ensuring secure, resilient satellite IoT communication requires addressing cybersecurity threats, including protecting against quantum computing-enabled attacks.
- Maintaining reliable wide-area connectivity in remote or harsh environments is difficult but critical to enabling autonomous vehicle functionality.
- The challenge of integrating multi-orbit satellite systems (GEO, MEO, LEO) for seamless service and network management.
- The requirement for long lifecycle and ruggedness of edge AI computing hardware to withstand extreme physical conditions.
- Need for zero-trust architecture and advanced encryption to counteract vulnerabilities in complex edge and network systems.
- Handling disconnections or failures in communication infrastructure while maintaining operational continuity for unmanned systems.
