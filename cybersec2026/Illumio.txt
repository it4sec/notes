# Summary of the Presentation

---

## Title

Containment, Zero Trust and the Security Graph - the path to Anti-Fragility

---

## Authors/Presenters

- Maj. General (Ret.) Viet Luong, Vietnamese Refugee, USC Graduate, 173rd Airborne, 82nd Airborne, 101st Airborne, Deputy Commanding General Eighth Army US Forces Korea, Commander US Army Japan, Entrepreneur and Veterans Advocate.
- Source reference to Forrester report by John Kindervag on Zero Trust Model of Information Security.

---

## Key Points

### Zero Trust Overview

- Zero Trust is a strategy designed to stop data breaches and prevent cyber-attacks by eliminating trust from digital systems.
- Emphasizes knowing: 
  1. Who the President (critical asset) is,
  2. Where the President is,
  3. Who should have access to the President.
- Shifts from perimeter-based protection to micro-perimeters and protect surfaces with controls and monitoring.

### Technical Foundations

- Euler Graph Theory (Seven Bridges of Königsberg Problem) is referenced as an analogy for non-linear relationships in security.
- The "First Hit Rule Policy" in traditional firewalls is highlighted as inefficient.
- AI Security Graph offers security observability by creating non-linear rules and mapping dedicated resources (nodes), traffic flow (edges), with properties (labels).

### Rule Writing and Policy Creation

- Introduces "Kipling Method of Zero Trust Rule Writing," focusing on Who, What, When, Where, Why, and How.
- Example rule based on identity, application validation, timing, workload location, etc.
- Emphasis on simplicity and focusing on the main point rather than overcomplicated defenses (quote from Frederick the Great).

### Military Analogies & Insights from Maj. General Viet Luong

- Use of military concepts like terrain, influence terrain, impact terrain, high ground, and bounded areas.
- Gen. Luong's favorite weapon and historic military maps emphasize strategic defense in depth and controlling blast radius.

### Zero Trust Deployment Methodology (5 Steps)

1. Define the protect surface
2. Map the transaction flows
3. Architect a Zero Trust environment
4. Create Zero Trust policy
5. Monitor and maintain

- Tailored, "antifragile" approach.

### Antifragility Concept

- Inspired by Nassim Nicholas Taleb: 
  - Beyond resilience or robustness,
  - Resilience resists shocks, antifragile improves from them.
- General Adaptation Syndrome analogy showing growth stronger after stress.

### Containing Blast Radius & Segmentation

- Segmentation rationale and enforcement emphasized.
- Highlights that all breaches or bad things occur inside "ALLOW" rules.
- Protection through micro-perimeters, segmentation gateways, and command and control.

### Zero Trust Learning Curve

- Protect surface sensitivity/criticality increases with time on the Zero Trust journey.
- Identified tiers from Learning to Crown Jewels to Tertiary assets.

---

## Important Conclusions and Insights

- Zero Trust represents a paradigm shift from traditional perimeter security to treating every asset as potentially vulnerable and removing implicit trust.
- Security Graphs and AI-driven dynamic mapping allow improved observability and enforcement.
- The military and graph theory analogies provide intuitive ways to understand network segmentation, control, and antifragility.
- Antifragility as a security goal drives continuous improvement and adaptation after attacks or stress.
- Proper segmentation and limiting blast radius inside an environment are critical to prevent lateral movement of threats.
- Effective Zero Trust requires continuous learning, policy adaptation, and tailored deployment to organizational priorities.

---

## Technical or Other Problems Emphasized

- Traditional firewall rules operate by "First Match Wins" which can cause security gaps.
- Cybersecurity perimeter defenses alone no longer suffice; attackers breach perimeters easily.
- "Soft chewy centers" in network architectures provide vulnerabilities exploited by attackers.
- Segmentation enforcement is complex and often weak, yet critical as all attacks happen inside "ALLOW" rules.
- Learning curve challenges organizations face to identify and protect their most critical assets ("crown jewels").
- Difficulty in creating non-linear, context-aware rules that adapt dynamically instead of static policies.
- Need for new tools like the AI Security Graph to understand and control resource relationships effectively.
- Maintaining and monitoring Zero Trust environments require continuous effort and adaptation.

---

This presentation strongly advocates for adopting Zero Trust architectures grounded in advanced AI-driven graph models, supported by military strategic thinking and adopting antifragility principles to improve cybersecurity postures in the modern threat landscape.
