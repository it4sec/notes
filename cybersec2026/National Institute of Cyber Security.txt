## Key Points

### What is Synthetic Data?
- Synthetic data is artificially generated based on the statistical properties of real data but contains no real individuals' information.
- It is not just fake data or noisy real data; instead, it produces “plausible but never actually existed” records.
- Proper synthetic data breaks direct and indirect links to original sensitive personal information, enabling strong privacy protection.
- Differential privacy or other anti-reidentification frameworks are often necessary to ensure compliance and privacy.

### Importance & Advantages of Synthetic Data
- Real data is becoming scarce and difficult to use due to privacy, legal, and practical limitations.
- Synthetic data is a key technology to overcome data scarcity for AI, especially in scenarios involving rare events or sensitive information.
- Synthetic data enables data sharing, model training, and simulating rare/extreme situations without exposing real personal data.
- It provides quantifiable privacy guarantees and compliance support more easily than other privacy-enhancing technologies (PETs).
- Synthetic data can be generated in common file formats, making it readily usable and distributable without special infrastructure.

### Privacy-Enhancing Technologies (PETs)
- PETs are categories of technologies that protect data during its lifecycle.
- Includes secure multiparty computation (MPC), homomorphic encryption (HE), federated learning (FL), trusted execution environments (TEE), differential privacy (DP), and synthetic data generation (SDG).
- Synthetic data with differential privacy (DP-SDG) is currently considered the most practical PET for many organizations.

### Quality and Evaluation of Synthetic Data
- Evaluation metrics: fidelity (how similar), utility (usefulness for tasks), and privacy (protection strength).
- Risk of reidentification addressed by analyzing singling out, linkability, and inference risks.
- Tools like PETsARD help quantitatively measure synthetic data risks and efficacy.

### Legal and Regulatory Landscape
- No outright prohibition of synthetic data, with active legal and regulatory development worldwide.
- Europe, East Asia (including Taiwan, Korea, Japan, Singapore, China), and the US have evolving policies supporting responsible synthetic data use.
- Differences in progress and approaches exist, but consensus recognizes synthetic data as a critical compliance tool.
- Synthetic data is distinct from deepfake content: responsible synthetic data generation emphasizes transparency, provenance, and legitimate use.

### Common Misconceptions Addressed
1. Synthetic data lacks legal support → debunked; many jurisdictions have explicit guidelines.
2. Synthetic data is risk-free for privacy → false; risks exist without differential privacy and careful design.
3. Synthetic data = fake data or deepfake → no, intents and transparency differ fundamentally.
4. Training AI purely on synthetic data leads to model collapse → avoidable with proper mixing of real data and verification.
5. Good cybersecurity equals privacy → No, privacy governance is distinct and requires additional measures.
6. Federated learning and confidential computing eliminate the need for synthetic data → incorrect, each solves different problems and should be used in combination.

### Practical Use Cases in Taiwan
- Government agencies synthesizing population and residence data for secure research.
- Financial sector leveraging synthetic data for fraud detection collaboration without compromising privacy.
- Policy foundations building trust and enabling innovation through synthetic data.
- Synthetic text generation enabling secure usage in high privacy domains like healthcare, finance, and social welfare.

## Important Conclusions and Insights

- Synthetic data is a foundational technology for unlocking AI potential while addressing scarcity and privacy concerns.
- Privacy protection in synthetic data is non-trivial and requires embedding mathematical guarantees like differential privacy.
- Real data will remain necessary as training data input—synthetic data complements rather than replaces it.
- Combining synthetic data with other PETs (like federated learning and confidential computing) creates a robust privacy-safe data ecosystem.
- Adoption of synthetic data technologies is growing globally with supportive legal frameworks emerging.
- Organizations should adopt thorough evaluation metrics and tools to balance fidelity, utility, and privacy.
- Responsible synthetic data generation emphasizes transparency, regulatory compliance, and ethical use, sharply contrasting with malicious deepfake technologies.

## Technical and Other Problems Highlighted in the Presentation

1. **Data Scarcity and Quality Challenges**  
   - Real data availability is declining due to privacy laws, data monopolies, and rarity of extreme events.
   - Collecting and labeling data is expensive, slow, and legally complex.

2. **Privacy Risk in Synthetic Data**  
   - Pure synthetic data generation risks memorizing and leaking rare outliers or sensitive samples.
   - Post-generation filtering strategies can create "swiss cheese" holes exposing real data indirectly.
   - Distance-based privacy measures at the dataset level may not protect the model itself from sophisticated extraction attacks.

3. **Model Collapse**  
   - Training models on synthetic data alone leads to loss of diversity, suppression of rare features, and overall degradation.
   - Complete abandonment of real data causes irreversible damage to model quality.
   - Requires careful mixing of real and synthetic data and human verification to avoid this collapse.

4. **Regulatory Ambiguity and Variation**  
   - Different countries and regions are at varying stages of regulating synthetic data and related privacy technologies.
   - Legal debates on pseudonymization vs. anonymization standards impact compliance and implementation.

5. **Misconceptions Leading to Misuse or Underuse**  
   - Incorrect beliefs about synthetic data legality, privacy, and capabilities hinder adoption.
   - Confusion between synthetic data and deepfakes damages trust and understanding.

6. **Incomplete Privacy Protection Without Differential Privacy**  
   - Only synthetic data with integrated differential privacy (DP-SDG) can mathematically guarantee privacy protections.
   - Without DP, synthetic data may leak private information encoded in model parameters.

7. **Limitations of Federated Learning and Confidential Computing Alone**  
   - These technologies do not solve data scarcity or imbalance.
   - They protect data flow and computation but not underlying data representativeness or privacy completely.
