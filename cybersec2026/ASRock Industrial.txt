# Summary of Presentation: "超越合規: IEC 62443 到 EU CRA 的AIoT 資安實踐"  
**Title:** Beyond Compliance: AIoT Cybersecurity Practices from IEC 62443 to EU CRA  
**Authors/Presenters:**  
謝斗文 (Hsieh Tou-Wen)  
東擎科技股份有限公司 (ASRock Industrial)  
Vice General Manager  

---

## Key Points

### 1. Introduction  
- Cybersecurity (資安) is now a fundamental requirement for enterprises to enter the market, especially for IoT and edge computing devices across manufacturing, retail, energy, logistics, and smart zones.  
- Cybersecurity is both a market-entry threshold ("入場門檻") and operational imperative ("關鍵保障") to ensure device long-term operation stability.  
- Secure products require balancing security and efficiency costs.

### 2. IEC 62443 Journey  
- Initial cybersecurity evaluations involved extensive, multi-dimensional questionnaires without clear standards or answers. This posed challenges to sustaining cybersecurity capability.  
- Companies often lack scalable cybersecurity capabilities and improperly rely on expert-driven or manual responses, hindering long-term product and market support.  
- IEC 62443 dual certification (IEC 62443-4-1 for secure development lifecycle & IEC 62443-4-2 for secure product) is identified as the most direct and rigorous method to embed security as a core competency.  
- Achieving cybersecurity involves threat modeling, vulnerability mitigation, rigorous testing, and audits to build true operational capability—not just conformance.

### 3. EU Cyber Resilience Act (CRA) Challenges  
- EU CRA represents a new phase, requiring continuous security responsibility beyond initial certification and shipment; certification is only the beginning.  
- Enterprises must maintain product cybersecurity throughout product lifecycle—including monitoring, vulnerability reporting, patching, and incident response within regulatory timeframes.  
- Legacy manual, document-heavy approaches are inadequate to meet CRA requirements. Automation and digitization are necessary.  
- CRA also demands detailed SBOM (Software Bill of Materials) management and dynamic vulnerability tracking across software stack layers (Apps, Drivers, OS, BIOS).  
- Automated vulnerability comparison and alerting systems are critical to shift from passive to proactive risk management.

### 4. Deep Defense Cybersecurity Platform  
- Security must extend beyond system-level to field deployment, covering the entire product lifecycle: deployment, runtime, maintenance.  
- ASRock Industrial’s platform includes:  
  - AiFDO for zero-touch secure deployment reducing operational risk from human error.  
  - AiSafeGuard for AI-based endpoint protection that enforces "zero trust" through proactive interception, not just reactive scanning.  
  - AiSMA for remote management, scalable to large device fleets with secure OOB (out-of-band) management protocols (e.g., Redfish).  
- This layered approach covers prevention, rapid vulnerability detection, incident response, and lifecycle security management.

### 5. Strategic Conclusions  
- Cybersecurity is evolving from a checkbox to a growth enabler and competitive advantage.  
- Enterprises must integrate security by default into product development and lifecycle operations.  
- Automation, AI assistance, and continuous risk monitoring form the foundation of scalable cybersecurity engineering for compliance and resilience.  

---

## Important Conclusions and Insights  
- Achieving IEC 62443 dual certification is necessary but no longer sufficient; the EU CRA requires ongoing cybersecurity responsibility.  
- Manual approaches are obsolete under CRA mandates, mandating automated workflows for compliance, vulnerability management, and incident handling.  
- SBOM management must become dynamic and automated, going beyond static inventory to actively support vulnerability correlation and patch prioritization.  
- AI and automation are critical to reduce manual effort and operational risks, enabling zero-touch deployment, real-time endpoint protection, and efficient large-scale remote management.  
- Deep defense platforms that integrate lifecycle security functions—deployment, runtime protection, and maintenance—are essential to mitigate real-world risks to edge computing devices.  
- Security should be viewed as an enabler of stable operations and market access, rather than a cost or barrier.

---

## Problems and Challenges Highlighted in Presentation

- **Lack of Standardized Answers:** Traditional customer cybersecurity questionnaires have no standard answers, creating uncertainty about capability.  
- **Manual, Document-Heavy Processes:** CRA compliance is hindered by reliance on offline documentation and manual workflows—error-prone and not scalable.  
- **Insufficient Operational Capability:** Many companies lack continuous security capability to operate through the product lifecycle under CRA expectations.  
- **Ineffective Vulnerability Management:** Traditional SBOM systems rely on manual lookup, leading to slow, inefficient vulnerability responses.  
- **Human-Dependent Deployment Risks:** Manual setup and configuration in field deployment introduce high time, labor costs, and increase security risk.  
- **Insufficient Runtime Protection:** Conventional endpoint defenses are reactive, leading to delayed virus/malware detection after attacks occur.  
- **Device Management Complexity:** Rapid growth in device fleets makes manual maintenance impossible, increasing cost and reducing resilience.  
- **Physical Security Gaps:** Physical access to devices remains a major risk often overlooked by network-centric defenses.  
- **Need for New Core Systems:** CRA demands new core cybersecurity systems (e.g., SBOM management, CRA lifecycle management) beyond legacy development-focused tools.
