## Key Points

### Company Background and Vision
- **HENNGE** is a company focused on solving various business-to-business (B2B) enterprise challenges.
- Founded in 1996, HENNGE transitioned to cloud services in 2011.
- Listed on the Tokyo Stock Exchange in 2019, established subsidiaries in Taiwan (2016) and the USA (planned for 2025).
- Vision centers on *Liberation of Technology* — enabling equal access to technological benefits.

### Cybersecurity Landscape and AI Impact
- AI accelerates advantages for attackers, introducing many new attack scenarios.
- Attackers benefit more from AI due to lower costs and exploitation of any defense weaknesses.
- Even a single vulnerability can lead to a security breach.

### Zero Trust as a Crucial Security Model
- Traditional models grant wide access once basic authentication is completed.
- Zero Trust enforces continual verification, restricting access to only authorized users, devices, and time-limited sessions.
- Zero Trust minimizes lateral movement risks within networks.

### VPN vs Zero Trust Network Access (ZTNA)
- Traditional VPNs have overly broad trust boundaries, making it easier for attackers to access internal resources.
- VPNs cannot finely control access by user, device, or time and conflict with Zero Trust principles because they trust the internal network by default.
- ZTNA authenticates every access attempt and limits network resource access to authorized entities only.

### Challenges in Implementing Zero Trust
- Integration complexity spans identities, devices, networks, and applications.
- User experience can be hindered by excessive authentication steps.
- Existing legacy systems may lack support for modern authentication protocols.
- Insufficient visibility into access logs and behavior analytics.
- Need for integrated, easy-to-deploy Zero Trust solutions.

### HENNGE Solutions
- **HENNGE Access Control (HAC)**: Cloud access control platform with Zero Trust at its core.
  - Supports major SaaS platforms like Microsoft 365, Google Workspace, Salesforce.
  - Features Single Sign-On (SSO), role/location-based access policies, device certificate trust, Secure Browser to prevent data leakage on mobile, and HENNGE Connect—a VPN replacement generating public URL access to internal servers.

- **HENNGE Connect** offers quick deployment without hardware costs.

- **HENNGE Mesh Network** technology:
  - Implements VPN at endpoints to reduce exposed attack surfaces.
  - Utilizes peer-to-peer mesh connections and micro-segmentation to block lateral movement.
  - Avoids centralized traffic bottlenecks ensuring optimal communication performance.

### Demonstrations and Visuals
- Illustrated access and login flows emphasizing user authentication and group-based resource permissions.
- Comparative visuals of VPN and ZTNA architectures pointing out trust boundaries and access controls.

## Important Conclusions and Insights
- AI-powered attacks are accelerating, making traditional perimeter-based security models obsolete.
- The Zero Trust security model, combined with innovative cloud-based access control platforms, effectively mitigates growing cybersecurity risks.
- Transitioning from VPNs to Zero Trust Network Access enhances security posture by limiting trust scopes, enforcing multi-factor continuous verification, and blocking lateral attack movements inside networks.
- Comprehensive cloud-native solutions like HENNGE's suite provide scalable, integrated, and user-friendly approaches to security transformation.
- Companies must prioritize the adoption of Zero Trust and modern access controls to defend against increasingly sophisticated AI-enhanced cyber threats.

---

## Technical and Other Problems Highlighted in the Presentation

### VPN Limitations and Risks
- Overly broad trust boundaries allow excessive access after connection.
- Lack of granular access control at user, device, and temporal levels.
- High risk of lateral movement by attackers within the internal network.
- Conflicts with Zero Trust principles since VPNs predetermine internal network trust.
- Difficult integration, user compliance challenges, and compatibility issues with legacy systems.
- Poor visibility into access logs and user behavior hampers threat detection.

### AI-Driven Threat Landscape
- AI lowers the cost for attackers to identify and exploit vulnerabilities rapidly.
- Defenders have a structural disadvantage as attackers exploit any small weakness.
- Increased attack diversity and speed complicate traditional defense strategies.

### Implementation Challenges for Zero Trust
- Complexity in consolidating identity, device, network, and application control.
- Balancing security with user experience to prevent workflow disruption.
- Legacy infrastructure limitations impede adoption.
- Necessity for unified deployment solutions to streamline Zero Trust adoption.

This presentation effectively outlines the evolving cybersecurity challenges in the AI era, emphasizing the critical need for Zero Trust architecture supported by innovative solutions such as HENNGE’s cloud-based platforms and mesh networking technology to replace legacy VPNs.
