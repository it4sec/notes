## Key Points

### Challenges in OT Security
- OT environments face critical security challenges due to:
  - Legacy systems that cannot be updated
  - IoT devices with extremely limited computing resources
  - Increasing connectivity ("Everything is connected"), eliminating real physical isolation and expanding attack surfaces
  - Production uptime (yield) is critical; any security measures impacting device efficiency are often rejected.
  
### Traditional IT Security Model Limitations
- Traditional perimeter defense with a trust assumption is flawed
- Once breached, threats spread laterally (Lateral Movement) causing widespread damage in a flat, trusted OT network.

### Agent-based vs Agentless Security
| Aspect          | Traditional IT (Agent-based)                  | Modern OT (Agentless)                          |
|-----------------|----------------------------------------------|-----------------------------------------------|
| Performance Impact | High; may cause old devices to malfunction or lose warranty | Zero impact; does not affect device operation or performance |
| Applicability   | Limited to specific OS PCs or servers        | Covers all IoT and legacy machines without agent installation |
| Deployment Effort | Very high; each device requires individual downtime for installation | Very low; network-layer traffic analysis, plug and play |

### Strategic Shift: Control Layer Defense
- Move protection from passive monitoring to active constraint at the network control layer
- Zero trust micro-segmentation principles:
  1. No trust by default; require strict identity verification for every request
  2. Enforce least privilege access with strictly closed communication to only necessary resources
  3. Independently verify and continuously monitor traffic per IP to confine threats to single devices
  
### Micro-segmentation and Automation Challenges
- Manual maintenance of Access Control Lists (ACLs) across thousands of devices is resource-intensive and error-prone
- Automation through AI:
  - Intelligent behavioral analysis
  - Automated policy generation
  - Compliance verification

### Janus Control Layer Solution
- Pure agentless platform requiring no program installs on endpoints
- Transparent bridging with no changes to network architecture or IP config
- Supports diverse legacy IT, Windows, and specialized IoT devices seamlessly
- Deployment close to devices via integration with edge network switches and routers, providing a "net" for detection and blocking
- Efficient group protection managing 1-48 endpoints per protection node reduces deployment cost

### Key Benefits
- Minimizes reliance on system patches; protects even vulnerable or EOL (end-of-life) devices
- Extends lifespan of costly legacy production equipment in a secure state
- Complies with international standards like SEMI E187 for supply chain security
- Provides complete logs for security event auditing and SOC integration
- Provides granular control and automatic isolation of unnecessary services and ports

### Visualized Risk and Centralized Control
- Real-time risk scoring and distribution of device lists for clear visibility
- Log export for integration with existing enterprise SOC/SIEM for unified defense coordination

## Important Conclusions and Insights
- OT environments require modernized, zero-trust, micro-segmentation architectures to effectively contain advanced persistent threats (APTs).
- Traditional perimeter and agent-based security are largely inadequate or infeasible in OT due to performance and legacy system constraints.
- Automation and AI-driven policy generation are essential to overcome the enormous complexity and scale of OT networks.
- Control layer network protection offers a pragmatic and scalable method to maintain continuous operation with high security.
- Comprehensive network-level defense, including micro-segmentation and zero trust, greatly reduces risk without compromising production uptime.
- Implementing international standards such as SEMI E187 within the solution expedites compliance and market readiness.

---

## Technical and Other Problems Highlighted
- Legacy OT systems frequently run outdated, unpatchable software, making traditional endpoint security ineffective.
- IoT devices have limited compute resources preventing installation of security agents.
- Manual ACL management across thousands of endpoints is unrealistic and error-prone.
- The flat and trusted OT network architecture facilitates rapid lateral threat movement after perimeter breach.
- Security solutions that degrade equipment performance or require downtime directly impact production uptime, which is unacceptable in OT.
- Lack of real-time visibility and integration with enterprise security operations hinders effective cyber defense.
- Over-reliance on patching leaves OT vulnerable as many devices reach EOL or don't support updates.

---

This presentation thoroughly explores the challenges in operational technology (OT) cybersecurity and advocates an advanced, agentless, zero trust micro-segmentation approach implemented at the network control layer, emphasizing automation and AI intelligence to secure legacy and diverse OT environments with minimal operational disruption.
