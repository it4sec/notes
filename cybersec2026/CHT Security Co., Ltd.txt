## Key Points

### 1. Statistical Analysis of Security Incidents  
- Analyzed data for 2024 and 2025 showed consistency in cyberattack trends.  
- Major malware types: Loader (23%), Payload (19%), Backdoor (15%).  
- Top initial attack vectors: Exploitation of Public-Facing Applications, Valid Accounts misuse, Spearphishing Links.  
- Newly observed threat intelligence on Hash, Domain, IP increased significantly in 2025.

### 2. Security Risk Trends in 2025
- Publicly facing software and services remain big targets.  
- Legitimate account abuse emphasizes the importance of identity and access management.  
- Spearphishing attacks are increasingly sophisticated targeting individuals and enterprises.  
- Supply chain risks pose significant attack entry points through subsidiaries or partners.  
- Critical infrastructure, especially network devices and remote access, are under increasing attack.

### 3. Incident Trend by Industry  
- Growing incident response (IR) cases from manufacturing, healthcare, and government sectors.  
- Cloud environments and network communication equipment show a rising trend in attacks from 2018 to 2025.

### 4. Case Studies on Cloud Supply Chain Security Incidents  
- Case 1: Ransomware attack on core operational system in a hybrid cloud environment involving lateral movement facilitated by poor RDP controls and system vulnerabilities.  
- Case 2: A cloud supply chain upstream provider was compromised, resulting in multiple client tenants being impacted through effective use of valid credentials and exploitation of partner relationships (GDAP abuse).

### 5. Identified Blind Spots in Taiwanese Traditional Industries Using Cloud  
- Loss of administrative control and management accounts.  
- Long-term neglect in system maintenance and patching.  
- Failures in remote access control, improper privilege management, lack of backup and cross-site backup.

### 6. Proposed Remediation for Incidents  
- Thorough removal of malware and related files.  
- Patch vulnerabilities and revise firewall/network architecture.  
- Deploy cybersecurity defenses and verify effectiveness.  
- Proactive monitoring and pre-launch system checks.  
- Implementation of Multi-factor Authentication (MFA), Cloud Security Posture Management (CSPM), Endpoint Detection and Response (EDR/MDR).

### 7. Hacker Attack Kill Chain and Modern Challenges  
- Traditional defenses fail at initial step as attackers use legitimate credentials.  
- Living off the Land (LOL) tactics to evade detection by abusing built-in tools.  
- Use of vulnerable drivers ("Bring Your Own Vulnerable Driver") for privilege escalation.  
- Final destructive phase: backup destruction and full encryption (ransomware).

### 8. Building Cybersecurity Immunity System  
- 24/7 SOC and MDR monitoring and incident response.  
- Active exposure and threat management through red team exercises and darknet intelligence.  
- Expert consulting on zero trust architecture and incident response capability enhancement.

## Important Conclusions and Insights

- Cloud supply chain attacks continue to escalate in sophistication and reach, threatening multiple industries.  
- Longstanding security blind spots in traditional industries amplify risk, especially in cloud integration.  
- Identity and access management, alongside continuous threat exposure management, are critical defensive pillars.  
- Multi-layered security strategies with proactive threat detection and incident response are imperative to build resilience.  
- Collaboration with experienced cybersecurity service providers and certified teams significantly enhances defense and recovery capabilities.

---

## Technical and Other Problems Emphasized

- **Exploitation of Public-Facing Applications:** The primary entry vector remains unpatched or vulnerable internet-facing applications.  
- **Legitimate Account Abuse:** Attackers rely heavily on valid credentials to bypass traditional security measures.  
- **Weak Remote Access Control:** Poor management and lack of access source control, especially for RDP, amplify attack surface.  
- **Supply Chain Vulnerabilities:** Insecure upstream cloud vendors or partners can lead to cascading effects on clients.  
- **Lack of Maintenance and Patch Management:** Long-term neglect increases vulnerability to exploitation.  
- **Insufficient Backup Strategy:** Missing offsite or diversified backups increases risk against ransomware.  
- **Living off the Land (LOL) Tools:** Use of built-in system tools for attacks complicates detection.  
- **Emerging Threats Using BYOVD:** Vulnerable drivers exploited for privilege escalation bypass many security defenses.  
- **Traditional Defenses Ineffectual:** Conventional perimeter defenses fail as attackers increasingly use legitimate access paths.  
- **Necessity for Continuous Monitoring:** 24x7 SOC/MDR and proactive exposure management are critical to early attack detection.

---

This report highlights the evolving cloud supply chain threat landscape and illustrates practical mitigation measures, emphasizing the urgency for enterprises to adopt comprehensive security frameworks to improve their cyber resilience.
