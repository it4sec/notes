## Key Points

### Agenda Overview  
- Challenges facing SMEs and midmarket due to enterprise-grade threats vs limited defensive resources  
- Shift for partners from hardware transactions to strategic platform collaborations  
- Introduction of SonicPlatform combining AI with zero trust security on a unified, cloud-managed architecture  
- How AI enhances zero trust with adaptive policies, segmentation, risk assessment, and automated incident response  
- Economic model for partners focusing on zero-touch deployment, recurring revenue, and deep customer retention  
- Vision and roadmap toward profitable growth and expansion in 2026 and beyond  

### Cybersecurity Landscape Challenges  
- Attack surfaces now borderless due to cloud expansion, remote work, and generative AI  
- AI-driven attacks expected to increase by 300% from 2024-2025  
- 83% of data breaches involve cloud or hybrid environments  
- Average data breach cost is $4.9 million  
- 43% of cyberattacks target SMEs, the fastest-growing and weakest defense segment  
- 60% of hacked SMEs shut down within 6 months  
- Over 4 million global cybersecurity job vacancies highlight talent shortage  
- Partners need a platform-based approach rather than recruiting  

### The Urgency of Now  
- Exploitation of vulnerabilities will shrink to under 24 hours in 2025, and under 60 seconds by 2026  
- Four converging pressures on customers: compliance demands, talent scarcity, AI-powered attacks, and proliferation of hacker tools  

### Internal AI Risks  
- Rapid spread of AI tools inside enterprises increases risks, especially unauthorized AI tools  
- Legacy cybersecurity does not address AI-native threats such as prompt injection, shadow AI, data poisoning, and AI supply chain vulnerabilities  

### Partner Business Model Transformation  
- Old model: hardware sales with limited stickiness and no recurring revenue  
- New model: strategic platform partnerships with unified SonicPlatform for multi-services, predictable MRR growth, and decade-long customer relationships  

### Zero Trust Fundamentals  
- Zero Trust means "never trust, always verify" with continuous authentication  
- Transition from old perimeter defense to identity as the new perimeter  
- Core principles: verify identity, assess posture, least privilege, micro-segmentation, continuous verification  
- Simplification across采购 (procurement), deployment, and ongoing management is key for scalability  

### SonicPlatform Architecture  
- Unified cloud management with AI-driven analytics  
- Key components: Firewall, SSE/SASE, ZTNA, Endpoint Detection, Unified Analytics, AI-Driven Security Operations Center (SOC), and multi-tenant cloud console  
- Supports zero-touch deployment and lifecycle automation resulting in easier scaling with less operational overhead  

### AI Capabilities on the Platform  
- Autonomous policy optimization removing shadow permissions  
- Predictive insights spotlight vulnerabilities for proactive engagement  
- Real-time compliance monitoring for frameworks like PDPA, NIS2, ISO 27001, CIS  
- Automated threat detection, containment, and forensic-enabled incident recovery  

### Roadmap to AI-Enhanced Zero Trust  
1. Assess identity, devices, and network posture; prioritize short-term wins  
2. Consolidate tools under SonicPlatform, centralizing control  
3. Deploy zero-touch policies based on least privilege and micro-segmentation  
4. Automate policy tuning and risk scoring within configured guardrails  
5. Expand coverage quarterly leveraging predictive insights and driving ARR growth

## Important Conclusions and Insights
- Zero Trust is no longer an aspirational ideal but a baseline security expectation for SMEs and midmarkets  
- AI is essential to realize scale and adaptability in Zero Trust without increasing headcount  
- SonicPlatform’s single-policy framework integrating endpoint, network, and cloud defense creates efficiency and control  
- Partners who embrace platform-driven, AI-augmented cybersecurity delivery will achieve longer customer relationships and recurring revenue  
- 2026 is pivotal for customers to move from piecemeal solutions to secure scalable digital expansion

## Technical and Other Problems Highlighted

### Cybersecurity Challenges  
- Threat landscape has zero margins due to cloud, remote work, and AI enhancements for attackers  
- Shortening exploitation windows require faster detection and response  
- AI tools inside enterprises pose insider risks, especially unauthorized or shadow AI deployment  
- Existing firewall and DLP tools do not comprehend or protect AI-driven data flows, creating blind spots  

### Business Challenges for Partners  
- Talent shortage severely limits traditional hiring to address growing threats  
- Previous transactional hardware sales models lack sticky recurring revenue and meaningful customer engagement  
- Complexity in deploying and managing multiple disconnected security tools hinders operational efficiency  

### Complexity vs Simplicity  
- Complexity is identified as the enemy, with simplification of procurement, deployment, and management as a central guiding principle  

### AI Governance and Risk Controls  
- AI actions are confined within partner-defined policy guardrails  
- High-risk changes require explicit partner or customer approval  
- Explainability and audit trails are critical for trust and compliance  

---

This presentation emphasizes the critical role of AI-driven Zero Trust security to transform SME and midmarket cybersecurity. It advocates a unified, simplified, and scalable platform approach for partners to sustainably address evolving threats and business realities in 2026 and beyond.
