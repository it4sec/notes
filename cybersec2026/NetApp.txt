## Key Points

### The Rising Threat of Ransomware
- Global ransomware attack frequency has surged by 132%.  
- Average business downtime due to attacks is 21 days.  
- Average disaster recovery cost reaches $1.82 million.  
- 75% of enterprises directly suffer business and revenue losses.

### Limitations of Traditional Layered Defense
- Perimeter defense often breached.  
- Static backups fail to halt damage spread.  
- No guarantee of 100% attack protection.  
- Data storage systems need to become proactive defense cores.  
- Goals: Resilience at the data storage level, zero data loss, real-time detection, and rapid response.

### Active Cyber Resilience Approach
- Going beyond merely blocking attacks to minimizing operational impact.  
- Real-time detection and response before data loss occurs.  
- Immediate blocking of abnormal behavior to cut off attack sources.  
- Rapid recovery with full visibility and data for optimizing security posture.

### NetApp’s Technological Edge
- Market-leading “safest storage on the planet.”  
- Built-in security in design, covering data, applications, identity, network, and perimeter.  
- Post-quantum cryptography (PQC) integrated into storage devices to defend against future quantum computing threats.  
- Industry-unique NSA CSfC certification for storing top-secret data.  
- AI-powered ransomware protection combined with immutable snapshots and end-to-end encryption.

### Zero Trust Security Architecture for Storage
- Never trust, always verify approach.  
- Assumes breach and enforces least privilege access.  
- Multi-Admin Verification (MAV) and Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for strict access control.  
- Write Once Read Many (WORM) snapshots prevent data tampering.  
- AI-driven behavior anomaly detection and automated ransomware interception.

### Comprehensive Ransomware Resilience Framework
- Single control plane (NetApp Console) managing all workloads and integrating with existing security ecosystems.  
- Automated threat identification, protection, detection, response, and recovery with AI and immutable data safeguards.  
- Guided, fast recovery workflows reduce recovery time objective (RTO) and prevent reinfection.

### Wide Infrastructure Compatibility & Hybrid Cloud Defense
- Supports diverse workloads (file systems, virtualization, containers) across leading cloud platforms (AWS, Azure, Google Cloud) and on-premises systems.  
- Flexible backup targets and seamless edge-to-cloud integration.

### Automated & Immediate Threat Mitigation
- Tamper-proof snapshots created automatically within seconds of threat detection.  
- Immediate locking of attacker accounts to contain infection source.  
- Automatic feeding of threat intelligence to SIEM/SOAR systems for centralized monitoring.

### Partnerships Enhancing Cyber Resilience
- Collaborations with Commvault and Elastio for end-to-end automated ransomware recovery and backup verification.  
- Closed-loop recovery with AI-driven threat detection and verified clean backups.

### Continuous Readiness & Compliance
- Non-disruptive readiness drills simulate full ransomware attack response and recovery to ensure business continuity readiness and regulatory compliance.

### Flexible Pricing & Trial
- Subscription options from 12 to 36 months available.  
- 30-day free trial with full features unlocked, no storage limits, and no upfront commitment.

## Important Conclusions and Insights
- **NetApp’s Cyber Resilience strategy pivots beyond traditional defense, emphasizing proactive data storage-level resilience combined with AI and immutable technology.**  
- The integration of post-quantum cryptography future-proofs sensitive data against emerging quantum threats.  
- Automated threat detection and remediation drastically reduce response times and operational downtime.  
- The solution architecture supports diverse modern IT environments, from on-premise systems to hybrid and multi-cloud ecosystems.  
- Strong partnerships with backup and verification solutions enhance data integrity and recovery confidence.  
- Flexibility in deployment and pricing adapts to varying enterprise needs and budgets.  
- Continuous testing and readiness ensure that organizations are prepared to face inevitable cyberattacks with minimal disruption.

## Technical/Other Problems Emphasized in the Presentation

- **Increasing frequency and sophistication of ransomware attacks** leading to long downtime and costly recovery expenses.  
- **Traditional layered security and static backup solutions are no longer sufficient** to protect critical data and prevent loss/spread of attacks.  
- **Cyber attackers exploiting gaps in storage security and leveraging future quantum computing capabilities** to compromise encrypted data.  
- **Lack of real-time detection and immediate response capabilities** allows damage escalation before mitigation.  
- **Insufficient recovery mechanisms** result in extended recovery time, operational interruptions, and financial loss.  
- **Challenges with managing security across hybrid and multi-cloud environments**, requiring seamless interoperability and consistent protection strategies.  
- **Risk of insiders or stolen admin credentials deleting backups** without immutable protections.  
- **Need for automation and AI to handle complex, fast-moving cyber threats** beyond human operational capacity.  
- **Regulatory compliance pressures** require demonstrable proof of cybersecurity readiness through drills and audits.
