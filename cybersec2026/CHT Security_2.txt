# Summary of Presentation: Cyber Innovation – Reshaping Network and Endpoint Threat Hunting Defense

### Title
**Cyber Innovation: Reshaping Network and Endpoint Threat Hunting Defense**  
Presented at CYBERSEC 2026 Taiwan Cybersecurity Conference

### Author/Presenter
- Ruei Liu (劉叡), Director, Cyber Innovation  
- CHT Security (中華資安國際), subsidiary of Chunghwa Telecom Group

---

### Key Points

- **Rapid Lateral Movement Threat (2025 Forecast):**  
  The fastest global hacker lateral movement is expected to take only 27 seconds to move from one endpoint to another, reflecting a rapidly shrinking window for effective detection and response.

- **Company Overview:**  
  CHT Security, Taiwan's first publicly listed cybersecurity company (listed Sept 8, 2025), has decades of expertise and a large skilled workforce (>370 employees, 98% with bachelor or higher education). It provides end-to-end cybersecurity services including prevention, detection, response, and forensic recovery.

- **Awards & Credentials:**  
  The company has received continuous 7 years of top grades (all "A") in government cybersecurity vendor evaluations, achieved international certifications (ISO 27001, 27701, 20000, 17025, IEC 62443), and won multiple industry awards.

- **Current Cyber Threat Landscape:**  
  - Average hacker dwell time globally has worsened (from 11 days in 2024 to 14 days in 2025).  
  - Lateral movement time average is shrinking drastically, with a record of 27 seconds reported.  
  - 82% of breaches involve "malware-free" attacks using legitimate tools and credentials (Living off the Land - LoTL).  
  - Average global breach costs are declining but remain high (US$4.44M, with US enterprises at US$10.22M).  
  - Taiwanese enterprises face daily cyber attacks with major threats being loaders, payloads, backdoors, and spearphishing.

- **Five Major Local Security Risk Trends in Taiwan:**
  1. Public-facing application exploits  
  2. Abuse of valid accounts highlighting identity access management (IAM) needs  
  3. Spearphishing campaigns boosted by AI-generated phishing emails  
  4. Supply chain risks involving subsidiaries and partners  
  5. Critical infrastructure risks with a surge in cloud and network devices being targeted (+500% and +550%)

- **Failures of Traditional Perimeter Defense:**  
  - Assumption that "perimeter = security" is obsolete due to cloud, VPN, SaaS, and modern identity models.  
  - Malware-based detection is ineffective against 82% of attacks with no malicious files.  
  - Single-layer tools cannot provide full attack visibility, leading to incomplete detection.

- **Two Real-World Infrared Incident Response (IR) Cases:**
  1. **Critical Network Device Intrusion:**  
     Long-term stealthy compromise of a router module with disabled audit logging and hidden backdoors, allowing attackers full control bypassing endpoint detection visibility.  
  2. **Strategic Network Pre-Positioning in Critical Infrastructure:**  
     Use of Living off the Land (LoTL) techniques with solely Windows built-in tools, stealthily extracting credentials (NTDS.dit) to establish persistent footholds for future attacks.

- **Integrated Defense Approach: "Network + Endpoint + SOC" Triad:**  
  - SecuTex NP (Network Packet) for full packet capture and east-west traffic monitoring with local threat intelligence.  
  - SecuTex ED (Endpoint Detection) focusing on lightweight, rapid full endpoint scans (15-25 minutes), YARA rule-based detection to fill sandbox blind spots, and compliance monitoring.  
  - SOC platform integrating network and endpoint data with automation, playbooks, and cross-layer correlation enabling detection accuracy above 99%.

- **Quantified Defense Improvements Post-Implementation:**  
  - Mean Time to Detect (MTTD) reduced from 11 days to 2 hours (-99%)  
  - Mean Time to Respond (MTTR) reduced from 73 days to under 24 hours (-98%)  
  - False positive rates decreased by 70%  
  - SOC labor effort reduced by 55%  
  - Potential breach cost savings over $1.9M USD

- **Recommendations for CISOs:**  
  - Conduct Red Team exercises to assess ATT&CK framework coverage.  
  - Address east-west blind spots with Network Detection and Response (NDR) solutions.  
  - Integrate MTTD/MTTR as board-level KPIs for security resilience.

- **Closing Summary:**  
  Network and endpoint combined threat detection is more effective than either alone (1+1=99). Passive SOC operations increase risk of prolonged undetected attacks. Proactive threat hunting is crucial.

---

### Important Conclusions and Insights

- Traditional detection technologies focused on perimeter and malware signatures are failing dramatically against modern, fast-moving, credential-based, malware-free attacks.  
- Holistic, multi-layered defense with network and endpoint visibility correlated through an intelligent SOC is essential to reduce detection and response times to viable levels.  
- In Taiwan specifically, critical infrastructure, manufacturing, and medical sectors are under exponentially rising cyberattack pressures, demanding specialized focus in these areas.  
- Living off the Land (LoTL) tactics mean that defenders must detect complex behavioral and timing anomalies rather than rely on signature-based detection alone.  
- Investment in automation, threat intelligence correlation, and playbooks dramatically reduces SOC workload and increases SOC efficiency and accuracy.

---

### Technical and Other Problems Emphasized in the Presentation

- **Challenges in Visibility:**  
  Lack of east-west network visibility and reliance on endpoint-only monitoring leaves large blind spots for threat actors.  
- **Speed of Adversarial Actions:**  
  Lateral movement measured in seconds impacts defenders' ability to contain attacks before severe damage.  
- **Malware-Free Attacks:**  
  Attacks executed solely via legitimate system tools bypass conventional antivirus and EDR solutions.  
- **Persistence in Network Devices:**  
  Attackers compromise trusted network infrastructure elements that evade endpoint-centric defenses.  
- **Detection Blind Spots:**  
  Sandboxes misclassify dangerous files as low risk; without tailored YARA rules based on reverse engineering, critical threats remain undetected.  
- **SOC Operational Inefficiencies:**  
  Manual or uncorrelated alert handling increases false positives and prolongs incident response times.  
- **Supply Chain and Third-Party Risks:**  
  Attack vectors extending through partners and subsidiaries complicate perimeter and identity security efforts.  
- **Increasing Costs and Impact:**  
  Despite some cost reduction trends, data breaches remain extremely costly and damaging, especially to larger enterprises.

---

This presentation strongly advocates for a paradigm shift towards integrated, behaviorally aware, cross-layered defense systems backed by local threat intelligence and automated SOC workflows to effectively combat the evolution of cyber threats in the coming years.
