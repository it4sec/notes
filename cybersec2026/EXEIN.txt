# Summary of "The 24h Challenge"

## Title
The 24h Challenge: Focus on CRA & Introduce Runtime Protection

## Authors/Presenters
Lief Tang (唐稚筌) - Lead Solutions Engineer, Exein | APAC

---

## Key Points

- **Cyber Resilience Act (CRA) Overview**:
  - First horizontal EU cybersecurity regulation addressing all products with digital elements.
  - Effective from 10 December 2024.
  - Applies to manufacturers, importers, and distributors of digital element products in the EU market.
  - Penalties include up to EUR 15 million or 2.5% of worldwide annual turnover.
  
- **Compliance Milestones**:
  - 10 Dec 2024: CRA came into force.
  - 11 Jun 2026: Conformity assessment body notification due.
  - 11 Sep 2026: Reporting obligations under Article 14 begin, applying to ALL existing products on the EU market.
  - 11 Dec 2027: Full CRA compliance mandatory.

- **Product Classification**:
  - Categories range from Default (~90%) to Critical.
  - Compliance assessment requirements vary: self-assessment, third-party assessment, or notified body depending on risk level.

- **Manufacturer Obligations (Article 13 + Annex I)**:
  - Identify and document vulnerabilities and component dependencies (including SBOM).
  - Coordinate vulnerability disclosure policy.
  - Provide timely remediation and secure update distribution.
  - Publicly disclose fixed vulnerabilities.
  - Support products with updates for at least 5 years.
  
- **Challenges in 24h Reporting**:
  - Requirement to report exploited vulnerabilities within 24 hours (Article 14).
  - Challenges include lack of accurate SBOMs, fragmented device fleets, manual reporting bottlenecks, blind spots in third-party components, and absence of runtime visibility.
  - Manual triage of CVEs takes days, not meeting the 24h requirement.

- **Runtime Security as a Solution**:
  - Essential for detecting active exploits on deployed devices in real time.
  - Automates alert triage and reporting workflows.
  - Provides evidence and allows virtual patching within minutes.
  - Supports continuous compliance, not just periodic audits.

- **Exein Solutions**:
  - **Exein Analyzer**: For pre-market compliance, generates SBOMs, scans CVEs, analyzes vulnerability reachability without needing source code.
  - **Exein Runtime**: On-device monitoring, behavioral anomaly detection, policy enforcement, continuous security assessment, and evidence generation.
  - Combo facilitates full lifecycle CRA compliance from firmware analysis to runtime protection.

- **Automated 24h Workflow**:
  - Detect → Triage → Mitigate → Report within 24 hours using combined Analyzer and Runtime data.
  - Example scenario: CVE published at 2AM; full reporting completed with 17 hours to spare.

- **Compliance Evidence Documentation**:
  - SBOMs, CVE scan reports, incident logs, timestamped submission records, and continuous monitoring reports.
  - Compliance requires ongoing evidence, not a one-time certification.

- **Cost and Risks of Non-Compliance**:
  - Market access risk (withdrawal from EU market, corrective actions).
  - Financial penalties up to €15 million or 2.5% annual turnover.
  - CRA compliance as a competitive advantage, especially for APAC manufacturers targeting Europe.

- **Practical Advice**:
  - Reporting obligations start in 4 months (Sep 2026).
  - Must automate workflows; manual triage reporting is insufficient.
  - Start with what you have (SBOM + runtime monitoring).
  - Runtime monitoring is required for compliance and zero-day detection.
  - Utilize platforms that ship with SBOMs validated by tools like Exein Analyzer.

---

## Important Conclusions and Insights

- The CRA represents a groundbreaking shift in EU cybersecurity regulation by mandating swift vulnerability reporting and continuous security evidence maintenance.
- Manual methods for vulnerability triage and reporting cannot meet the demanding 24-hour window, especially with complex embedded/IOT device ecosystems.
- Runtime security solutions paired with modern firmware analysis tools are essential to achieve compliance and maintain security post-market.
- The CRA enforcement will intensify starting September 2026, affecting all products currently on the EU market.
- There is significant risk in non-compliance including financial penalties, product withdrawal, and brand damage.
- Early adoption and automation of CRA-required security workflows offer a market opportunity to differentiate and secure enterprise customers.

---

## Problems Emphasized in the Presentation

- **Complexity in Vulnerability Management**:
  - Difficulty in tracking hundreds of open-source components within embedded firmware.
  - Gaps in Software Bill of Materials (SBOM) completeness and accuracy.
  - Deep transitive dependencies of third-party components that manufacturers must monitor.

- **Fragmented Device Fleets**:
  - Multiple firmware versions across thousands of deployed devices complicate impact assessment and remediation.

- **Manual Processes and Lack of Automation**:
  - Vulnerability reporting is often slow and bureaucratic.
  - Lack of automated workflows compromises the ability to meet the 24-hour reporting obligation.

- **Lack of Runtime Visibility**:
  - No real-time detection of exploitation attempts on devices leads to reliance on external sources for vulnerability awareness.
  - Without runtime monitoring, recognition of exploitation events is reactive, not proactive, which is non-compliant.

- **Insufficient Evidence for Compliance**:
  - Compliance requires continuous evidence, which traditional periodic audits cannot provide.
  - Incident and vulnerability evidence must be automatically generated and documented.

- **Liability on Integrators for Third-Party Components**:
  - Manufacturers must exercise due diligence on third-party software components, including verifying security and reporting upstream vulnerabilities.

---

This presentation strongly advocates for combining static firmware analysis with real-time runtime protection to meet the Cyber Resilience Act obligations efficiently and effectively.
