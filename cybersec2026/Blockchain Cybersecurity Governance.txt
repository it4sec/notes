**AI時代司法聯盟鏈：區塊鏈資安治理與智慧監控實踐**  
*AI Era Judicial Alliance Chain: Blockchain Cybersecurity Governance and Intelligent Monitoring Practice*

## Key Points

- **Digital Era Challenges**  
  The AI era is described as both a digital era and an era of widespread skepticism, particularly concerning the authenticity and trust of digital evidence in judicial proceedings.

- **Digital Evidence & Trust**  
  - Over 90% of criminal cases in London Police involve digital evidence.
  - Digital evidence cannot be physically touched or directly verified by the naked eye; it is stored electronically and must be read and analyzed using devices.
  - The concept of "Digital Trust" is critical for demonstrating that today's digital files are the same as those seized yesterday (especially cloud evidence).

- **Forensic Principles**  
  The presentation emphasizes the cross-law-and-technology nature of digital forensics:
  - Law demands "real evidence"
  - Technology provides "scientific validation"
  
- **Challenges in Digital Evidence**  
  - Digital evidence is vulnerable to forgery and alteration.
  - Without effective preservation and verification mechanisms, evidence reliability suffers, leading to increased litigation and disputes.
  
- **Chain of Custody Issues**  
  - There are triple dilemmas in the custody chain of digital evidence: collection, preservation, and verification/authentication.
  - Proper procedural requirements and verification of "identity" of digital evidence are prerequisite for admissibility in court.

- **Judicial Alliance Chain (Blockchain Application)**  
  - Introduced by Taiwan’s Ministry of Justice Investigation Bureau in 2019.
  - Blockchain technology developed to address the preservation and authenticity challenges of digital evidence.
  - The Judicial Alliance Chain includes five founding members: Judicial Yuan, Ministry of Justice, High Prosecutors Office, Investigation Bureau, Police Department.
  - Uses Hyperledger Fabric consortium blockchain for decentralization, transparency, and sustainability.
  - Evidence is hashed and recorded on-chain to ensure unaltered preservation.

- **Governance and Certification**  
  - The "b-JADE" certification mark ensures node and organizational compliance, operational standards, and personnel training.
  - Chaincode (smart contracts) undergo rigorous testing and validation to prevent bugs and security vulnerabilities.

- **Technical Implementation and Monitoring**  
  - Multiple layers of security defenses: test environment blockchain, smart contract VV&T (verification, validation, and testing).
  - Intelligent monitoring includes machine learning to detect anomalies related to RAFT consensus mechanism or DDoS attacks.
  
- **Future Outlook and Digital Governance Framework**  
  - Emphasis on creating an innovative digital governance framework combining forced track retention, human autonomy, transparency, accountability.
  - Judicial Alliance Chain is not just a national evidence treasury but a foundational infrastructure to rebuild judicial digital trust in the AI era.
  - Aligns with international legal developments on AI accountability, transparency, and certification.

---

## Important Conclusions and Insights

- The integration of blockchain technology into judicial digital evidence management helps solve critical issues of trust, chain-of-custody, and proof of authenticity.

- The decentralized architecture of Judicial Alliance Chain enhances sustainability and security over traditional centralized evidence management.

- Formal governance and certification processes (b-JADE) ensure technical compliance and operational integrity.

- The approach reduces redundancy in court hearings by providing real-time joint verification platforms, saving judicial resources.

- Combining legal requirements with technical rigor (testing, smart contract vetting, AI-powered anomaly detection) forms a multi-layer secured architecture for judicial digital evidence.

- Taiwan’s implementation is one of the world’s first to integrate legal and blockchain technology in this comprehensive manner.

---

## Identified Technical and Other Problems Emphasized

- **Digital evidence vulnerability**  
  Digital data can be easily copied, altered without trace, challenging the ability to prove "sameness" or identity of evidence over time.

- **Traditional custody chain limitations**  
  Paper-based records cannot technically guarantee non-tampering of digital evidence throughout its lifecycle.

- **High verification costs**  
  Manual expert testimony for every digital evidence hash value is resource-intensive and costly.

- **Smart contract risks**  
  Potential security vulnerabilities in chaincode, including global variables, randomness, timestamp manipulation, and map iteration order, can affect stability and correctness.

- **Consensus mechanism attacks**  
  RAFT consensus in Hyperledger Fabric can be targeted by attacks causing leader election anomalies or log replication delays.

- **Anomaly transaction and traffic monitoring**  
  Detection of DDoS or malicious smart contract calls is necessary but challenging in a decentralized blockchain environment.

- **Legal-technical knowledge gap**  
  The need for legal professionals to understand technology and technical staff to grasp legal requirements is critical but difficult to implement effectively.

- **AI-related governance challenges**  
  Ensuring human autonomy, transparency, fairness, accountability, and privacy in AI systems that interact with judicial processes.
