# Summary of the PDF Presentation

## Title  
**Financial Operational Resilience on Cybersecurity Ecosystem Blueprint (FORCE-B)**  
金融資安韌性發展藍圖  
Date: May 7, 2025

---

## Authors / Presenters  
- Financial Supervisory Commission, R.O.C. (Taiwan)  
- Collaboration with Financial Information Sharing and Analysis Center and Taiwan Institute of Economic Research  

---

## Key Points  

### 1. Background & Policy Progress  
- Overview of Taiwan's financial cybersecurity policy timeline:  
  - 2020.08: Financial Cybersecurity Action Plan 1.0  
  - 2022.12: Financial Cybersecurity Action Plan 2.0  
  - 2024.07: Introduction of Zero Trust Architecture guidelines for financial sector  
  - 2025.12: Launching of Financial Cyber Resilience Development Blueprint with 4 major components and 10 priority actions  

### 2. National Security and Cybersecurity Strategic Alignment  
- "Cybersecurity is national security" (2025 Vision)  
- Emphasis on:  
  - Zero Trust Architecture (ZTA)  
  - Post-Quantum Cryptography (PQC) development and migration  
  - AI security and trustworthiness  
  - Supply chain visibility and safety  
  - Critical infrastructure resilience and third-party cybersecurity exercises  

### 3. Global Trends in Cybersecurity Regulation  
- Enhancing senior leadership accountability and maturity  
- Shifting cybersecurity left (early integration) and managing software components  
- AI cybersecurity governance and PQC preparedness  
- Strengthening supply chain management and operational resilience  

### 4. Four-Axis Resilience Governance Framework  
- A. Governance with clear accountability  
- B. Comprehensive protection from design to technical resilience  
- C. Cross-domain ecosystem defense and intelligent intelligence sharing  
- D. Solid resilience ensuring continuity and rapid recovery  

### 5. Ten Key Priority Actions  
- Strengthen executive governance and responsibility  
- Enhance talent cultivation and cross-institution knowledge exchange  
- Integrate cybersecurity early into software design  
- Promote Zero Trust Architecture deployment  
- Improve cybersecurity monitoring, detection, and response effectiveness  
- Forward-looking technology deployment (AI, PQC)  
- Supply chain risk management and ecosystem strengthening  
- Intelligence sharing and cooperative defense  
- Cyberattack simulations and incident response exercises  
- Multi-level backup and redundancy for critical financial services  

### 6. Technical Enhancements and Tools  
- Adoption of Software Bill of Materials (SBOM) and vulnerability tracking  
- API security baseline development  
- Cloud security monitoring standards for AWS, Azure, and GCP platforms  
- Container security monitoring frameworks  
- Deployment of advanced AI-driven cybersecurity defense and automation tools  
- Use of MITRE ATT&CK and MITRE Engage frameworks for attack simulation and defense  

### 7. Emerging Technology Focus  
- AI security threat landscape and defense mechanisms based on MITRE ATLAS  
- PQC migration strategy addressing encryption agility and risk prioritization  
- Collaborative approach between industry and regulators for quantum threat mitigation  
- Encourage proactive vulnerability disclosure and supplier risk grading  

### 8. Organizational Culture and Ecosystem  
- Embed cybersecurity into corporate governance and ESG principles  
- Frequent cybersecurity exercises involving simulation and real-world attack techniques  
- Foster cyber tactical defense mindset via training and scenario-based drills  
- Ecosystem-level coordination through information sharing and incident response collaboration  

---

## Important Conclusions and Insights  
- Cybersecurity must evolve from simple prevention to resilient, rapid response and recovery.  
- Embracing new technologies (AI, PQC, Zero Trust) is crucial for future-proof defense.  
- Cross-domain and ecosystem-level collaboration are essential to combat increasingly sophisticated threats.  
- Continuous refinement based on empirical attack simulations underpins a mature cybersecurity posture.  
- Strong executive governance and clear accountability accelerate organizational resilience.  
- Supply chain security and transparency (including SBOM) directly impact overall financial ecosystem security.  
- AI introduces both opportunities for enhanced defense and new, complex attack surfaces requiring dedicated governance.  

---

## Technical and Other Challenges Highlighted  
- **Active Directory Security Risks**: Large percentage of enterprises have insecure AD CS configurations enabling attackers to gain domain admin rights within minutes.  
- **Post-Quantum Cryptography Transition**: Complexity in PQC migration requiring multi-faceted strategy covering technical readiness, risk assessment, resource planning, and international coordination.  
- **Supply Chain Risk Management**: Need for tiered supplier security assessments and collaborative information sharing to counter emerging supply chain threats.  
- **AI-driven Attack Vectors**: Emerging risks from AI and generative AI agents that can automatically craft exploits or social engineering attacks at machine speed.  
- **Cybersecurity Monitoring Gaps**: Continuous updates needed for detection rules to cover evolving attacker tactics, techniques, and procedures (TTPs).  
- **Zero Trust Implementation Complexities**: Policy, technical integration, and cultural shifts required to fully implement ZTA across diverse identity, device, network, application, and data domains.  
- **Incident Response and Resilience Testing**: Sophisticated scenario-driven exercises are necessary to validate preparedness and improve response workflows.  
- **Cloud and Container Security**: Expanding cloud-based security monitoring to cover hybrid environments, container platforms, and SaaS security poses operational challenges.  
- **Data Protection & Access Control**: Enforcing least privilege, dynamic attribute-based access, and encrypted data storage with real-time monitoring is critical but complex.  
- **Organizational and Talent Gaps**: There is a pressing need to upgrade security personnel capabilities and cross-domain collaboration frameworks to address fast-moving threats.
