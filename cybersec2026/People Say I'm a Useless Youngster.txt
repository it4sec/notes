# Summary of PDF Presentation

## Title
They Say I Am a Useless Young Person  
(他們說我是沒有用的年輕人)

## Author/Presenter
PD Lee  
Freelancer, Cybersecurity Vagabond (自由工作者, 資安流浪漢)

## Key Points

- **Agenda**  
  - Useless Young Person  
  - Catching Up with the Tail Lights  
  - Domain Expansion  

- **Personal Background and Career Path**  
  - Graduated from a management department  
  - Worked as a computer lab assistant and data center technician  
  - BBS campus site administrator and founding manager of management department site  
  - Difficulty finding engineering jobs at first  
  - After one year, still struggled to find work  
  - Eventually found contract work, then full-time work after two years  
  - Continuous learning and sharing of research progress  
  - Over 10 years later, remained humble, discovered APT events  
  - Became keynote speaker at APT symposium, honored as model employee but received minimum raise  
  - Moved overseas for language learning, self-exile, and migrant worker experience  
  - Returned after 10+ years, re-encountered a vicious cycle and started own business  

- **Self-Assessment and Strategies for Progress**  
  - Recognize disadvantage at starting point; catch up by making greater progress and leveraging different advantages  
  - Find correct learning channels:  
    - Passive learning: complete formal education, attend professional training courses  
    - Active learning: identify personal learning styles and focus on topics of interest  
  - Use of non-IT related AI courses to gain fresh perspectives and inspiration  
  - Recommended to fill educational gaps to avoid job discrimination and have solid foundational training  
  - Choose reputable training classes and instructors  

- **Learning Approaches and Practical Experience**  
  - Attend lectures, teach others, engage in practical battles  
  - Practice on oneself and attack hackers  
  - Improve hacking techniques  
  - Shared examples of phishing and hacking improvements with video demonstrations (YouTube reference)  

- **Domain Expansion and Interdisciplinary Integration**  
  - Combine original strengths with newly learned technical skills  
  - Bridge romanticism from liberal arts with practicality and technical ability from IT  
  - Leverage business thinking  
  - Address AI's influence responsibly—avoid AI FOMO, focus on complementary strengths rather than repetitive tasks  

- **Case Study and Examples**  
  - A demo case illustrating the combination of skills across domains  
  - Discussion of business impact through IDOR vulnerability in e-commerce  
  - Emphasis on competitor advantage when accessing future discount information, not consumers' access  

- **AI Influence**  
  - Rapid technical changes but slower societal changes  
  - Emphasize mutual benefit between human expertise and AI capabilities  
  - Visual example of personalized hair analysis consultant report displaying different styles and recommendations  

- **Closing Thoughts**  
  - Recommend filling educational credentials when possible  
  - Travel when facing life hurdles for broader perspectives and inspiration  

---

## Important Conclusions and Insights

- Starting without IT background is challenging and involves fighting inherent disadvantages  
- Continuous, diversified learning targeting one’s own style is crucial to keeping up and progressing  
- Cross-domain skills (liberal arts + technical + business) enhance problem-solving and adaptability  
- AI should be embraced wisely as a complementary tool, not a source of fear or redundancy  
- Practical, hands-on learning including hacking/debugging habits profoundly benefits cybersecurity professionals  
- Travel and life experience can unlock new insights and growth opportunities  

---

## Technical or Other Problems Highlighted

- Difficulty securing engineering or cybersecurity jobs with a non-IT academic background  
- Job market discrimination based on education background and credentials  
- Challenges of career stagnation despite contributions (low raises, underappreciation)  
- The vicious cycle of returning overseas-experience professionals facing similar systemic issues upon return  
- Need to keep pace with rapidly changing technical landscapes in cybersecurity and AI  
- Risk of falling into AI FOMO or replicating repetitive tasks that do not add unique value  
- Real-world vulnerabilities such as IDOR in e-commerce systems impacting business competitiveness  
- Importance of robust practical skills for defending against phishing and sophisticated hacking threats  

---

This presentation advocates perseverance, continuous upskilling tailored to oneself, an interdisciplinary mindset, and wise AI use to transform perceived "uselessness" into valuable cyber talent.
