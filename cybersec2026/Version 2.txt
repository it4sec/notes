## Key Points

### 2026 Cybersecurity Landscape in Asia-Pacific and Taiwan
- **Generative AI Attacks:** Deepfake/AI scams in Asia-Pacific increased by 1530%, with AI-generated phishing and deepfake attacks growing by 40%.
- **Taiwan's Pain Points:** Supply Chain attacks remain the top threat; 70% of Taiwanese enterprises’ CISOs report significant cybersecurity workforce shortages.
- **MTTR Crisis:** Traditional cybersecurity takes 15-20 days on average to detect and remediate intrusions, which is too slow in the AI era.

### Dual Challenges for Taiwanese Enterprises
- Normalized high-volume cyberattacks (2.63 million daily intrusion attempts with 6% annual growth).
- Supply chain attack vulnerabilities impact 90% of factories; huge talent gap with an 80,000-person shortage in cybersecurity talent.
- Existing teams are insufficient to handle growing sophisticated threats.

### Collapse of Traditional Defense - AI Era MTTR Crisis
- Incident repair times stagnate at 15-20 days without automation.
- Alert fatigue: over 50% of alerts unhandled due to overwhelming volume.
- Increasing average ransomware ransom demands exceeding $1.25 million.

### Emerging Threats
- Rapid attack speeds reduced from days to minutes.
- Mobile and remote work devices introduce significant security vulnerabilities.
- IT teams face alert fatigue with thousands of prioritized messages daily leading to missed critical alerts.

### Solution Vision: Transition from Manual Defense to “Self-Healing” Joint Defense
- **Detection:** Rely on ESET MDR’s AI-driven behavioral analysis and 24/7 expert monitoring beyond signature-based detection.
- **Operations:** Automate response using Atera AI-driven patch and script management.
- **Control:** Ensure endpoint compliance and physical isolation with Scalefusion MDM.

### The "Golden Triangle" of Self-Healing Defense
- **ESET MDR:** 24/7 multi-layered EDR telemetry and expert threat hunting.
- **Atera:** AI-generated automation scripts for patching and system health.
- **Scalefusion:** Conditional access, kiosk lockdowns, and endpoint compliance enforcement.

### Case Study: 5-Minute Closed-Loop Incident Response
- Detection of abnormal external IP access.
- Expert confirmation and endpoint network isolation.
- Automated patch deployment across vulnerable devices.
- Scalefusion enforced compliance lockdown until remediation is verified.

### Impact Comparison: Traditional vs. Self-Healing Defense
- Traditional method took 48 hours involving manual notification, onsite checking, logs inspection, and system reinstall.
- Self-healing approach cuts this to under 15 minutes via automated detection, isolation, and AI-driven remediation.

### 2026 Enterprise Cybersecurity Recommendations
- Use outsourced professional MDR monitoring instead of in-house overburden.
- Embrace AI automation to reduce manual operational delays.
- Enforce comprehensive endpoint controls ensuring no blind spots in mobile or IoT device management.

---

## Important Conclusions and Insights
- The traditional cybersecurity defensive paradigm is no longer sufficient for the AI-empowered threat landscape, which operates on minutes rather than days.
- There is an urgent need to adopt AI-powered MDR, automated operational tools, and stringent endpoint controls to build a resilient, self-healing cybersecurity posture.
- Workforce shortage demands reducing dependency on human analysts by leveraging AI-driven detection and automated patch management.
- Enterprises must embrace a fully integrated self-healing system combining real-time detection, automated remediation, and endpoint compliance enforcement to prevent damage escalation.

---

## Technical or Strategic Problems Highlighted
- **MTTR Delays:** Average time to detect and remediate threats remains painfully long (15-20 days), making organizations vulnerable.
- **Alert Fatigue:** Excessive false and low-risk alerts overwhelm IT staff, leading to missed critical warnings.
- **Rapid Attack Evolution:** Zero-day exploits and polymorphic malware generated using AI shorten attack windows to under 10 minutes.
- **Boundary Disappearance:** Remote work and mobile device proliferation create unmonitored attack vectors (shadow IT, lack of MDM).
- **Security Talent Shortage:** Taiwanese cybersecurity workforce gap of 80,000 severely limits threat response capabilities.
- **Traditional Patching Delays:** Over 30% of attacks originate from unpatched vulnerabilities, emphasizing the need for automated, zero-touch patching.
- **Compliance Gaps:** Without continuous device monitoring and conditional access enforcement, endpoints become vulnerable gateways.

---

This presentation stresses the transformation required from reactive security to proactive, AI-enabled, and automated defensive operations to combat evolving cybersecurity threats effectively.
