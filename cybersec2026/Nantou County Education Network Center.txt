# Summary of Presentation

## Title
**OPNsense Suricata SmartNIC: High-Performance Network Protection in Practice**

## Author / Presenter
* 鄭明彰 (Ming-Chang Cheng)  
  Nantou County Education Network Center  
  System Team / Non-traditional career / Elementary School Teacher  

## Key Points
- **Core Values of the Architecture:**
  - Controllability: Focus on mastering the protection logic, not just purchasing equipment.
  - Scalability: Relying on hardware stacking alone is insufficient for high-speed traffic handling.
  - Observability: Data forms the basis for future defense through advanced analysis, tracking, and feedback.
  - Evolvability: Use controllable technologies to build long-term evolving defense capabilities rather than one-time products.
  
- **Educational Network Environment Challenges:**
  - Distributed equipment across multiple schools.
  - High usage by staff and students.
  - Complex management due to dual-layer architecture (central + school ends).
  - Budget and manpower limitations.
  - Various security challenges like malware, IoT devices, NAT, DNS over HTTPS (DoH), QUIC protocol reducing visibility, ad blocking policies, and a lack of threat intelligence sharing.

- **Traffic Growth and System Pressure:**
  - Network backbone capacity growing from 2G to over 100G.
  - Firewall (FW), Intrusion Detection/Prevention Systems (IDS/IPS) CPU load continuously rising.
  - Performance limits of traditional software firewalls under increasing DPI costs.

- **System Architecture and Layered Defense:**
  - Backbone end handles FW, IPS, Network Packet Broker (NPB), Bypass.
  - School end handles FW, DNS, IDS, Micro-Sensor.
  - Use Layer 3/4 control for most traffic, leave Layer 7 (application layer) for critical flows.
  - Use flow steering and data for cost reduction and improved observability.
  - Indicators of Compromise (IOCs) feed back to firewall policies (IP/domain blocking).

- **Use of OPNsense and Suricata:**
  - OPNsense: Open source FreeBSD-based firewall platform.
  - Suricata: Multi-core DPI engine for deep packet inspection and threat detection.
  - ET (Emerging Threats) ruleset use for optimized detection.
  - Performance tuning essential for firewall throughput.

- **SmartNIC Integration:**
  - Offload high-speed packet capture, flow classification, and steering to hardware.
  - Reduce CPU burden by implementing blocking decisions in SmartNIC hardware (wDrop).
  - Support for 10G, 25G, 40G, 100G speeds for sustainable scaling.

- **Management and Monitoring:**
  - API-driven centralized management for configuration, firmware updates, backups, and alert integration.
  - Use Graylog, Vector, OpenSearch for security log aggregation, analysis, and real-time alerting.
  - IOC dashboards for visualization and operational decision-making.
  - Incorporate threat intelligence (CTI) and blocklists for proactive defense.

- **False Positive Handling and Operational Stability:**
  - Passlists and rule set simplifications reduce false alarms.
  - Immediate log analysis to understand blocking reasons and enable quick exceptions.
  - Maintain operational control to confidently block malicious traffic.

- **Future Directions:**
  - Lightweight Network Detection and Response (NDR).
  - Data-driven Security Operations Center (SOC) expansion.
  - Micro-Sensor enlargement for internal anomaly visibility.
  - Increased cross-unit CTI sharing and automated defense foundations.

## Important Conclusions and Insights
- This is not a one-time deployment but a sustainable, evolving architecture balancing performance, cost, and maintenance.
- Resiliency arises not from individual devices but from data-driven continuous operation, monitoring, and feedback.
- Extracting IOCs and feeding them back to defense equipment are crucial.
- Without data, there is no Artificial Intelligence-based SOC.
- The architecture lays the foundation for future threat intelligence sharing and automated protection.

## Technical and Other Problems Highlighted
- **Increasing Traffic Volume and System Load:**
  - School networks and backbone traffic are rapidly growing (up to 100G+), creating performance bottlenecks.
  - Traditional firewall and DPI solutions face CPU overload and throughput degradation.

- **Visibility Challenges:**
  - Use of DoH and QUIC protocols reduces the ability to inspect traffic at Layer 7.
  - Distributed, heterogeneous school environments complicate centralized management and observability.

- **Budget and Manpower Constraints:**
  - Many schools have limited budgets and staffing for comprehensive security deployment.
  - Managing multiple dispersed devices is complex.

- **False Positives and Detection Accuracy:**
  - High false positive rates require careful rule tuning and whitelist management.
  - Operational confidence requires understanding and controlling blocking decisions.

- **Legacy Network Architectures:**
  - Traditional architectures cannot scale efficiently to handle current and future traffic speeds.
  - DPI is costly in terms of CPU cycles, limiting inline full-traffic inspection.

- **Lack of Threat Intelligence Sharing:**
  - Incidents are reported but threat intelligence sharing mechanisms are lacking, reducing overall situational awareness.

- **Need for New Processing Approaches:**
  - High-speed inspection demands offloading and acceleration technologies like SmartNICs.
  - Suricata and OPNsense alone cannot handle all traffic at ultra-high speeds efficiently.

- **Internal Anomaly Detection:**
  - Backbone monitoring misses internal network anomalies.
  - Micro-Sensors at school level are proposed to cover these blind spots.
