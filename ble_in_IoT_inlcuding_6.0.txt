# Evolution of Bluetooth Technology: BLE in the IoT Ecosystem

**Authors:** Grigorios Koulouras, Stylianos Katsoulis, Fotios Zantalis  
**Journal:** Sensors (2025), 25, 996  
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.3390/s25040996  
**Date Read:** 2026-06-20

## One-Sentence Summary

This review surveys the evolution of Bluetooth technology with a focus on Bluetooth Low Energy (BLE), explaining how successive BLE releases—from version 4.0 to 6.0—have transformed BLE into a foundational connectivity layer for Internet of Things (IoT) systems.

## Research Motivation

The rapid evolution of BLE and the release of Bluetooth 6.0 created a gap in the literature: existing surveys do not comprehensively cover recent BLE capabilities, security mechanisms, mesh networking, sustainability implications, and emerging AI-enabled applications.

## Main Contributions

- Presents a historical overview of Bluetooth from versions 1.0 through 6.0.
- Compares Bluetooth Classic and BLE technical characteristics.
- Reviews BLE protocol evolution across versions 4.0–6.0.
- Summarizes BLE applications across healthcare, industrial IoT, smart cities, retail, wearables, automotive systems, and smart homes.
- Discusses implementation challenges including power management, coexistence, scalability, security, and interoperability.
- Connects BLE development with United Nations Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs).
- Identifies future research opportunities involving AI, edge computing, security, hybrid networking, and sustainable device design.

## What Is New in This Review

- One of the first comprehensive surveys to include **Bluetooth 6.0** features.
- Integrates recent capabilities such as:
  - Channel Sounding
  - Decision-Based Advertising Filtering
  - Monitoring Advertisers
  - LE Audio
  - Periodic Advertising with Responses (PAwR)
  - LE 2M 2BT PHY
- Extends traditional BLE surveys by explicitly linking BLE evolution with sustainability goals (SDGs).
- Provides a multidisciplinary perspective spanning smart cities, industrial automation, healthcare, retail, and automotive applications.
- Highlights convergence between BLE, AI/ML, edge computing, and hybrid communication architectures.

## Key Technical Insights

### BLE vs. Bluetooth Classic

| Feature | BLE | Bluetooth Classic |
|---|---|---|
| Power Consumption | Ultra-low | Moderate |
| Throughput | 125 kbps–2 Mbps | Up to 3 Mbps |
| Channels | 40 × 2 MHz | 79 × 1 MHz |
| Topologies | P2P, Broadcast, Mesh | Primarily P2P |
| Target Use Cases | Sensors, wearables, IoT | Audio, file transfer |

### Important BLE Milestones

- **BLE 4.0 (2010):** Low-energy protocol introduced.
- **BLE 4.2 (2014):** IPSP support and improved privacy.
- **BLE 5.0 (2016):** LE Coded PHY, 2 Mbps mode, extended range.
- **BLE 5.1 (2019):** Direction finding (AoA/AoD).
- **BLE 5.2 (2019):** LE Audio and Isochronous Channels.
- **BLE 5.4 (2023):** PAwR and Electronic Shelf Label support.
- **BLE 6.0 (2024):** Channel Sounding, enhanced filtering, lower latency.

## Representative Performance Characteristics

- Operating band: 2.4 GHz ISM
- Channel count: 40
- Data rates: 125 kbps, 500 kbps, 1 Mbps, 2 Mbps
- Typical range: 10–100 m
- Long-range mode: Up to ~400 m under ideal conditions
- Security: AES-128 encryption
- Network support: Point-to-point, broadcast, mesh

## Challenges Identified

- Power management trade-offs
- Coexistence with Wi‑Fi and other 2.4 GHz technologies
- Scalability in dense deployments
- Security and privacy risks
- Interoperability issues
- Range limitations
- Mesh network complexity

## Future Research Directions

- Quantum-resistant BLE security
- AI-assisted resource management
- Edge AI for local analytics
- Hybrid BLE + Wi‑Fi + LoRa architectures
- Self-healing mesh networks
- Advanced energy harvesting
- Sustainable hardware design
- Centimeter-level indoor positioning

## Key Takeaways for Researchers

1. BLE remains the dominant short-range, low-power connectivity technology for IoT.
2. Bluetooth 6.0 signals a transition toward context-aware and location-aware communication.
3. Future innovation will depend on integrating BLE with AI, edge computing, and hybrid networking.
4. Security, interoperability, and sustainability are becoming as important as throughput and energy efficiency.
5. Long-range BLE and mesh networking create opportunities for large-scale industrial deployments.
