# Executive Summary

This systematic literature review (SLR) investigates the growing threat of **GNSS spoofing and jamming attacks against unmanned aerial vehicles (UAVs)**.

The study analyzes how current research addresses security threats affecting UAV navigation and communication systems, focusing on:

* Spoofing detection
* Jamming mitigation
* GNSS-denied navigation
* Alternative localization
* Machine learning approaches
* Blockchain-based security

The authors conclude that although numerous detection and mitigation strategies exist, most solutions continue to rely on the same vulnerable infrastructure—namely GNSS and wireless communications.

The review identifies a critical research gap:

> Current solutions address symptoms rather than root causes.

Environmental resilience, mission completion metrics, and intentional denial-of-service scenarios remain significantly underexplored.

---

# Research Questions

The review is organized around three questions:

1. How do GNSS-based UAV systems protect themselves against security threats?
2. How effective are existing mitigation approaches?
3. What limitations remain in current solutions?

---

# Why This Study Matters

Modern UAVs increasingly support:

* Agriculture
* Cargo transportation
* Surveillance
* Military operations
* Disaster response
* Remote sensing

Most UAV platforms depend heavily on GNSS technologies such as:

* GPS
* GLONASS
* Galileo
* BeiDou

Because GNSS signals are weak and open by design, they are highly susceptible to manipulation.

Compromising navigation can result in:

* Mission failure
* Hijacking
* Loss of aircraft
* Data theft
* Operational disruption

# Threat Landscape

## GNSS Spoofing

Spoofing occurs when attackers transmit counterfeit GNSS signals to manipulate UAV positioning, velocity, and timing information.

### Consequences

* Route deviation
* Unauthorized landing
* UAV hijacking
* Mission manipulation

---

## GNSS Jamming

Jamming disrupts communication between GNSS satellites and UAV receivers.

### Jamming Categories

* Continuous jamming
* Reactive jamming
* Random jamming
* Constant jamming

### Consequences

* Signal loss
* Denial of service
* Mission termination
* Reduced operational range

---

# Security Approaches Identified

The review groups mitigation strategies into four categories.

---

## 1. Intrusion Detection and Prevention (IDP)

### Techniques

* Rule-based IDS
* Distributed IDS agents
* Statistical anomaly detection
* Kullback-Leibler divergence (KLD)
* Kalman filters

### Advantages

* Early threat detection
* Low deployment complexity

### Limitations

* High false-positive rates
* Performance overhead
* Dependence on GNSS availability

---

## 2. Machine Learning and Deep Learning

### Algorithms Identified

* Support Vector Machines (SVM)
* Artificial Neural Networks (ANN)
* Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM)
* Convolutional Neural Networks (CNN)
* Generative Adversarial Networks (GAN)
* Dynamic ensemble selection

### Features Used

* Signal-to-noise ratio (SNR)
* Carrier phase
* Doppler shift
* Pseudorange
* Satellite count
* OFDM parameters

### Representative Examples

| Method | Application                  |
| ------ | ---------------------------- |
| LSTM   | Flight trajectory prediction |
| CNN    | Satellite image matching     |
| GAN    | Adversarial IDS              |
| SVM    | Spoofing classification      |

### Key Insight

Multiple classifier systems outperform single-model approaches.

---

## 3. Alternative Localization

Alternative navigation approaches reduce dependence on GNSS.

### Technologies

* Ultra-wideband (UWB)
* IMU/INS
* Radar localization
* Cellular positioning
* Wireless ranging
* LiDAR

### Advantages

* GNSS independence
* Improved resilience

### Limitations

* Infrastructure requirements
* Environmental sensitivity
* Scalability challenges

---

## 4. Vision-Based Navigation

### Techniques

* SLAM
* Optical flow
* Stereo vision
* Satellite image matching
* Landmark localization

### Advantages

* Effective in GPS-denied environments

### Limitations

* Sensitive to lighting conditions
* Performance degradation in fog, rain, or darkness
* High computational requirements

---

## 5. Blockchain-Based Security

### Applications

* Secure data sharing
* Authentication
* Integrity verification
* Geolocation validation

### Technologies

* Ethereum
* Smart contracts
* Byzantine fault tolerance
* Distributed ledgers

### Advantages

* Immutability
* Traceability
* Decentralized trust

### Limitations

* Scalability
* Latency
* Environmental dependence
* Susceptibility to jamming

---

# Comparative Analysis of Existing Approaches

| Approach                 | Strengths         | Weaknesses                |
| ------------------------ | ----------------- | ------------------------- |
| IDS/IDP                  | Fast detection    | Resource intensive        |
| Machine Learning         | High accuracy     | Requires large datasets   |
| Blockchain               | Strong integrity  | Scalability issues        |
| Vision Navigation        | GNSS independence | Weather sensitivity       |
| Alternative Localization | Redundancy        | Infrastructure dependency |

# Major Research Gaps

The review identifies five persistent gaps.

## 1. Root Cause Analysis

Most studies focus on detection rather than eliminating vulnerabilities.

---

## 2. Environmental Resilience

Few studies consider:

* Rain
* Fog
* Cloud cover
* Terrain effects
* Electromagnetic interference

---

## 3. Mission Performance Metrics

Most work ignores:

* Mission success rate
* Flight duration
* Energy consumption
* Operational cost

---

## 4. Scalability

Solutions often assume:

* Single UAVs
* Limited geographic coverage
* Stable communication links

---

## 5. Intentional Service Denial

Current studies rarely consider:

* Service provider denial
* Multi-jammer scenarios
* Coordinated attacks

---

# Key Insights for Researchers

## Insight 1

GNSS dependence remains the core vulnerability across almost all UAV navigation systems.

---

## Insight 2

Machine learning improves detection accuracy but does not solve signal availability problems.

---

## Insight 3

Blockchain enhances trust and data integrity but cannot prevent physical-layer attacks.

---

## Insight 4

Vision-based systems are promising but lack environmental robustness.

---

## Insight 5

Hybrid navigation systems combining multiple sensors offer the best path toward resilient UAV operation.

---

# Recommended Research Directions

The authors recommend:

* Root-cause-focused security design
* Mission-oriented evaluation metrics
* Multi-sensor fusion architectures
* GNSS-independent navigation
* Adaptive detection algorithms
* Real-world testing environments
* Cross-disciplinary collaboration
* Standardized security benchmarks

# Practical Implications

The findings are relevant to:

* UAV manufacturers
* Aviation regulators
* Defense organizations
* Critical infrastructure operators
* Autonomous logistics providers

The study highlights that secure navigation should be treated as a system-level challenge rather than a standalone detection problem.

---

# Limitations of the Review

* Focused on studies published between 2016 and 2024
* Restricted to English-language literature
* Limited evaluation of real-world deployments
* Performance metrics were inconsistently reported across studies

---

# Key Takeaways

* Spoofing and jamming remain major threats to UAV navigation.
* Existing countermeasures largely depend on vulnerable GNSS infrastructure.
* Machine learning and blockchain are promising but insufficient on their own.
* Environmental resilience is significantly underexplored.
* Future systems require hybrid, multi-modal navigation architectures.
