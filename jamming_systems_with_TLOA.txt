# TLOA: A Power-Adaptive Algorithm Based on Air–Ground Cooperative Jamming

**Authors:** Wenpeng Wu, Zhenhua Wei, Haiyang You, Zhaoguang Zhang, Chenxi Li, Jianwei Zhan, Shan Zhao  
**Journal:** *Future Internet*, Volume 18, Article 81 (2026)  
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.3390/fi18020081  
**Keywords:** communication jamming, resource scheduling, air–ground networks, cooperative jamming, cognitive jamming, power adaptation

**Date Read:** 2026-06-20

---

## Executive Summary

This paper introduces the **Transmission Loss Ordering Algorithm (TLOA)**, a deterministic resource-scheduling approach for air–ground cooperative jamming.

The core objective is to maximize **jamming system operating duration** while maintaining effective disruption of dynamic air–ground communication networks.

Unlike reinforcement learning and heuristic optimization approaches that rely on iterative search, pre-training, or extensive environmental interaction, TLOA transforms the jamming problem into a **static deterministic optimization problem** using electromagnetic propagation models and cognitive estimation of communication behavior.

Simulation results show that TLOA can extend system operating duration by up to **41.6%** compared with baseline methods under ideal channel conditions while substantially reducing scheduling latency.

---

## Research Problem

Existing jamming resource scheduling methods face several limitations in air–ground environments:

- Large numbers of communication nodes and jammers
- Dynamic transmission power adjustment by adversaries
- Sparse and heterogeneous jammer deployment
- Limited battery capacity
- Complex electromagnetic propagation environments
- High-dimensional decision spaces
- Lack of reliable datasets for machine learning approaches

Most prior work focuses on:

- Radar jamming
- Static resource allocation
- Small-scale scenarios
- Single-jammer optimization

These assumptions do not generalize well to distributed air–ground jamming.

---

## Proposed Method: TLOA

TLOA operates in three stages:

1. Randomly select communication nodes as jamming targets.
2. Rank candidate jammers according to **transmission path loss**.
3. Dynamically allocate jamming power based on estimated jammer-to-signal ratio (JSR).

The algorithm prioritizes jammers with lower transmission loss to reduce power consumption while maintaining effective jamming.

### Key Design Principles

- Deterministic optimization
- Cognitive estimation of communication parameters
- Path-loss-aware jammer selection
- Continuous power control
- Adaptive jammer activation
- Reduced decision dimensionality

---

## Mathematical Contributions

The paper develops:

- Air–ground communication network models
- Air–ground cooperative jamming models
- Path-loss models for LoS and two-ray propagation
- Quantized jammer-to-signal ratio (JSR) metrics
- System operating duration model
- Communication power adaptation model
- Jamming effect estimation framework

The optimization target is:

> Maximize total jamming duration while ensuring all communication nodes satisfy the required JSR threshold.

---

## What Is New in This Paper

- Introduces the first **path-loss ordering strategy** specifically designed for air–ground cooperative jamming.
- Converts a dynamic stochastic jamming problem into a static deterministic optimization problem.
- Proposes a cognitive framework that estimates communication power and jamming effectiveness from reconnaissance information.
- Integrates electromagnetic propagation knowledge directly into scheduling decisions.
- Eliminates the need for expensive iterative searches used by reinforcement learning and evolutionary algorithms.
- Supports continuous power scheduling rather than fixed jamming levels.
- Jointly optimizes jammer activation, power allocation, and scheduling order.
- Explicitly addresses heterogeneous airborne and ground jammer coordination.

---

## Core Innovation: Dynamic-to-Static Conversion

The most important conceptual contribution is the transformation of a dynamic problem into a deterministic one through:

### Cognitive Certainty

Instead of estimating instantaneous communication power, TLOA uses:

- Historical reconnaissance data
- Real-time electromagnetic sensing
- Maximum possible communication power estimates

This creates a stable optimization target.

### Decision-Time Decoupling

Rather than solving a new optimization problem at every time step, TLOA:

- Builds a static jammer allocation strategy
- Uses expected path-loss values
- Adds safety margins for uncertainty

This significantly reduces computational complexity.

## Algorithm Complexity

| Algorithm | Time Complexity |
|---|---|
| TLOA | O(n × m + m log m) |
| GA | O(G × N × D) |
| PMABCA | O(G × N × (D + Tlimit)) |
| DQN/DDQN | O(T × B × (D + H)) |
| SRSA | O(S × D) |

Where:

- n = number of communication nodes
- m = number of jammers

TLOA scales linearly with network size.

## Experimental Setup

The authors evaluate TLOA across four scenarios varying:

- Number of communication nodes
- Airborne versus ground jammer ratios
- Network density
- Electromagnetic complexity

Baselines:

- DQN
- DDQN
- PMABCA
- SRSA
- Genetic Algorithm (GA)

Evaluation metrics:

- First decision time
- Decision latency
- System operating duration

---

## Key Results

### Operating Duration Improvements

Compared with benchmark methods, TLOA increases operating duration by:

- 41.6% (Scenario 1)
- 102.3% (Scenario 2)
- 82.2% (Scenario 3)
- 2827.3% (Scenario 4)

### Decision Speed

TLOA requires:

- 11.9–26.9 ms for initial decisions
- 5.9–24 ms per scheduling step

Deep reinforcement learning approaches require:

- Up to 10 seconds for initial deployment
- Up to 1 second per scheduling cycle

---

## Results Under Non-Ideal CSI

The authors evaluate all methods using:

- ±2 dB channel uncertainty
- Random path-loss fluctuations

Findings:

- All methods degrade under imperfect CSI.
- TLOA maintains superior scheduling speed.
- TLOA remains computationally robust despite estimation errors.
- Non-ideal CSI is identified as a major open challenge.

---

## Key Insights for Researchers

### Domain Knowledge Matters

Electromagnetic propagation models significantly improve decision efficiency.

### Deterministic Methods Remain Competitive

In time-critical environments, deterministic approaches can outperform learning-based methods.

### Decision Dimensionality Is the Bottleneck

Reducing the optimization space is more impactful than increasing model complexity.

### Cognitive Estimation Is Underexplored

Future systems should integrate:

- Signal source identification
- Communication power estimation
- Jamming effect prediction

### Robustness to CSI Errors Is Essential

Future research should address:

- CSI uncertainty
- Online adaptation
- Robust optimization

## Practical Examples

| Challenge | TLOA Strategy | Benefit |
|---|---|---|
| Dynamic communication power | Maximum power estimation | Stable optimization |
| Sparse jammer deployment | Path-loss ordering | Energy savings |
| Limited battery capacity | Adaptive activation | Longer operation |
| Complex propagation | LoS + two-ray models | Better realism |
| Unknown communication behavior | Cognitive estimation | Reduced uncertainty |
| Large-scale networks | Deterministic scheduling | Faster decisions |

---

## Limitations

The authors acknowledge several limitations:

- Assumes relatively accurate channel state information
- Uses simplified propagation models
- Does not address disconnected jammer networks
- Requires prior knowledge of communication node locations
- Does not model complex terrain effects
- Limited real-world validation

---

## Future Research Directions

- Robust scheduling under imperfect CSI
- Hardware-in-the-loop validation
- Multi-agent coordination with disconnected jammers
- Terrain-aware path-loss estimation
- Online communication node discovery
- Integration with reinforcement learning
- Deep learning for signal recognition
- Image-based electromagnetic environment analysis

---

## Key Takeaway

TLOA demonstrates that carefully designed deterministic algorithms can outperform reinforcement learning and evolutionary optimization approaches in dynamic air–ground jamming environments.

The paper suggests that future communication countermeasures should combine:

- Electromagnetic domain knowledge
- Cognitive sensing
- Deterministic optimization
- Robust uncertainty modeling

rather than relying solely on black-box machine learning.
