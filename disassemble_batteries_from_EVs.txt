# Intelligent disassembly of electric-vehicle batteries: a forward-looking overview

**Authors:** Kai Meng, Guiyin Xu, Xianghui Peng, Kamal Youcef-Toumi, Ju Li  
**Journal:** Resources, Conservation & Recycling, Volume 182 (2022)  
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1016/j.resconrec.2022.106207  
**Date read:** 2026-06-20

## Executive Summary

This review examines how artificial intelligence (AI) and machine learning (ML) can enable scalable, safe, and economically viable disassembly of end-of-life electric vehicle lithium-ion batteries (EV-LIBs). The authors argue that battery disassembly is the key bottleneck preventing circular battery value chains from reaching industrial scale.

The paper proposes a process-oriented taxonomy for intelligent disassembly spanning:

1. Disassembly preprocessing
2. Disassembly planning and decision-making
3. Disassembly operations
4. Human–robot interaction and collaboration
5. Design for disassembly (DfD)

The review synthesizes 123 publications published between 2010 and 2021.

---

## Research Questions

1. Why are AI/ML methods needed for EV-LIB disassembly?
2. How can AI/ML methods be applied across the disassembly workflow?
3. What are the major opportunities and challenges for intelligent disassembly?

---

## Why EV Battery Disassembly Matters

Fine-grained disassembly enables:

- Battery reuse and second-life applications
- Remanufacturing through component replacement
- Direct recycling with higher material purity
- Recovery of critical materials such as lithium, nickel, and cobalt
- Lower environmental impacts versus shredding-based approaches

Conventional shredding, pyrometallurgy, and hydrometallurgy introduce energy, contamination, safety, and greenhouse-gas trade-offs.

---

## Core Challenges Identified

### 1. Safety risks

- Residual charge and high voltage
- Thermal runaway and fire hazards
- Toxic electrolytes and gases
- Mechanical damage during disassembly

### 2. High design diversity

- Lack of standardized pack architectures
- Different module formats and joining methods
- Diverse thermal management systems

### 3. Uncertain end-of-life conditions

- Unknown state of health (SOH)
- Corrosion, deformation, and missing components
- Variable histories and degradation mechanisms

### 4. Robotic manipulation difficulties

- Adhesive bonding
- Flexible cables and connectors
- Dense packaging and restricted access
- Mixed joining technologies

### 5. Lack of lifecycle data

- Limited battery provenance information
- Poor interoperability across manufacturers
- Restricted data sharing due to intellectual property concerns

## AI/ML Applications Across the Workflow

### Intelligent preprocessing

Tasks:

- Battery identification
- State estimation (SOC, SOH, RUL)
- Classification and sorting

Representative methods:

- RFID-enabled tracking
- Computer vision with CNNs
- Support Vector Machines
- Gaussian Processes
- Neural networks
- Fuzzy inference systems
- Self-organizing maps
- K-means clustering

### Intelligent planning and decision-making

Tasks:

- Disassembly depth selection
- Sequence planning
- Task allocation
- Line balancing
- Reverse logistics optimization

Representative methods:

- Genetic algorithms
- Ant colony optimization
- Swarm intelligence
- Reinforcement learning
- Graph neural networks
- Attention mechanisms
- Deep combinatorial optimization

### Intelligent operations

Tasks:

- Component detection
- Screw and fastener localization
- Separation process optimization
- Robotic manipulation

Representative methods:

- Faster R-CNN
- Mask R-CNN
- YOLO
- Deep reinforcement learning
- Self-supervised learning
- Learning from demonstration
- Simulation-to-real transfer

### Human–robot collaboration

Tasks:

- Teleoperation
- Shared autonomy
- Dynamic safety monitoring
- Human action prediction

Representative methods:

- Recurrent neural networks
- Deep reinforcement learning
- Digital twins
- Cyber-physical systems
- AR/VR interfaces

### Design for disassembly

Objectives:

- Standardized interfaces
- Modular battery architectures
- Reduced adhesive use
- Improved traceability
- Embedded lifecycle information

## What Is New in This Paper

- First systematic review focused specifically on AI-enabled EV-LIB disassembly.
- Introduces a comprehensive five-stage taxonomy linking AI methods to disassembly activities.
- Connects battery prognostics with downstream recovery decisions.
- Highlights intelligent disassembly as a prerequisite for direct recycling.
- Integrates perspectives from robotics, battery science, sustainability, and circular economy research.
- Positions cloud platforms, blockchain, and digital twins as enabling infrastructure.
- Frames disassembly as a cyber-physical decision problem rather than a purely mechanical task.

---

## Key Insights for Researchers

### Insight 1: Battery health estimation is a disassembly problem

SOH, SOC, and RUL estimates directly determine:

- Recovery route selection
- Disassembly depth
- Economic value

Future work should optimize prognostics jointly with recovery decisions.

### Insight 2: Detection is not enough

Industrial systems require:

- Recognition
- State estimation
- Uncertainty quantification
- Decision support

End-to-end pipelines remain largely unexplored.

### Insight 3: Data scarcity is the dominant bottleneck

Critical needs include:

- Shared datasets
- Standardized battery labels
- Open ontologies
- Lifecycle data infrastructures

### Insight 4: Human–robot collaboration will dominate near-term deployment

Fully autonomous systems remain impractical because of:

- Safety constraints
- Product variability
- Dexterity limitations

Hybrid intelligence is likely to outperform full automation in the medium term.

### Insight 5: Design for disassembly is underexplored

Battery architecture choices strongly influence downstream recovery economics.

Future studies should integrate:

- Battery design
- Manufacturing
- Usage data
- End-of-life processing

## Research Gaps and Future Directions

- Benchmark datasets for EV-LIB disassembly
- Foundation models for industrial manipulation
- Explainable AI for safety-critical operations
- Multimodal perception systems
- Digital twins for closed-loop optimization
- Standardized battery passports
- Uncertainty-aware reinforcement learning
- Simulation environments for EV battery disassembly
- Sustainability metrics embedded in AI objectives
- Techno-economic validation at industrial scale

---

## Takeaway

The paper argues that intelligent disassembly is the missing link connecting EV adoption with truly circular battery supply chains.

The central message is that disassembly must evolve from fixed automation toward adaptive, data-driven, and collaborative systems that integrate battery diagnostics, perception, planning, and robotics.

Success will depend as much on data infrastructure, standardization, and battery design as on advances in AI algorithms.
