# Executive Summary

This study investigates the effectiveness of machine learning (ML) techniques for detecting **service supply chain attacks**, which exploit vulnerabilities in third-party vendors, software updates, and service providers.

Using the **DFRLab Software Supply Chain Security Dataset**, the authors evaluate three supervised learning algorithms:

* Decision Tree
* Random Forest
* XGBoost

The study demonstrates that ensemble methods significantly outperform traditional signature-based intrusion detection systems by enabling adaptive detection of evolving threats.

Among the evaluated models, **Random Forest achieved the best overall performance**, obtaining:

* **Accuracy:** 96.1%
* **Precision:** 96.4%
* **Recall:** 96.0%
* **F1-score:** 97.0%
* **ROC-AUC:** 0.96

The authors conclude that machine learning offers a scalable and effective approach for detecting supply chain attacks but acknowledge challenges related to computational cost and real-time deployment.

# Research Problem

Supply chain attacks increasingly target:

* Software vendors
* Cloud providers
* Third-party services
* Hardware manufacturers

Traditional security mechanisms such as:

* Signature-based IDS
* Firewalls
* Heuristic rules

often fail against:

* Zero-day attacks
* Advanced Persistent Threats (APTs)
* Multi-stage attacks
* Supply chain compromises

Recent incidents such as:

* SolarWinds
* Kaseya
* MOVEit
* NotPetya

demonstrate the limitations of conventional detection approaches.

# Supply Chain Attack Taxonomy

The authors identify four major categories of supply chain attacks.

## 1. Compromised Software Updates

Attackers inject malicious code into software releases.

### Example

* SolarWinds Orion attack

---

## 2. Hardware Supply Chain Attacks

Attackers tamper with hardware during manufacturing or distribution.

### Example

* Alleged malicious hardware implants in imported devices

---

## 3. Third-Party Service Provider Attacks

Attackers exploit vendor relationships.

### Examples

* Target HVAC breach
* MOVEit file transfer breach

---

## 4. Data Integrity Attacks

Attackers manipulate or destroy organizational data.

### Example

* NotPetya ransomware attack
